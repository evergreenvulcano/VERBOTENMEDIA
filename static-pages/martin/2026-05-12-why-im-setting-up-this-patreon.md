Why I’m setting up this Patreon

I am trying to raise roughly $100 per month to help cover the recurring tools and infrastructure that keep Verboten Media viable.

Verboten Media is an independent literary and experimental publishing project.

I am setting up this Patreon to support its continued survival and careful development — not as a content stream, but as a long-form literary project that has grown slowly, unevenly, and seriously over time.

If you want to support a strange, serious, independent literary project built around voice, collaboration, careful stewardship, and experimental publishing, this Patreon is one way to help.

Verboten did not begin as a social media presence or a publishing “idea.” It began with years of writing: a manuscript, fragments, notes, documents, physical artifacts, and a source-world shaped through collaborative, asynchronous work.

Roots

That history matters. The project’s DNA is not solitary content creation, but delayed exchange, parallel writing, accumulated fragments, shared pressure, and voices carrying each other forward over time.

Part of what keeps me with literature is that it can carry a discussion further than the people who began it. Across time, formats, friendships, arguments, and social change, writing can preserve forms of attention that are otherwise easy to lose. It is not made obsolete by entertainment systems or algorithmic value. At its best, literature remains a way of transmitting knowledge, difficulty, empathy, and unresolved human experience without forcing everything into immediate usefulness.

Verboten Media has already acted in the world as an enabling structure for literary work.

The earliest iterations of Verboten grew through the asynchronous Häggström/Nygren axis — a unique engine of literary voice, the death of conceptual punk, the surrealism of digital “reality,” and methodological agnosticism. These were vibrations of Swedish capital culture in the 2010s: literary ambition entangled with media work, agency logic, cultural precarity, and the Stockholm pressure to remain ironic while becoming professionally legible.

Alongside the manuscript, Verboten has also existed as a small literary platform. We have hosted events, helped authors distribute their work, shared our audience with musicians, and created situations where writing could circulate outside ordinary institutional channels. It is not a large organization, but it has already acted in the world as an enabling structure for literary work and life.

The current phase is stewardship.

For now, I am carrying the project forward: protecting the voices and conditions that made it possible, organizing the material, building the publishing surface, and finding ways to let the work become public without reducing it to something easier but less true.

Verboten Media is now more than a manuscript. It includes fiction, essays, publishing-house mythology, fragments, web infrastructure, and a long attempt to understand how complex creative material can survive contact with form.

The rising tide of barely floating dreams

The immediate goal of this Patreon is modest but meaningful: roughly $100 per month to help cover recurring tools that keep the project viable, including organizational infrastructure and limited AI-assisted editorial support where it helps preserve continuity rather than replace writing.

This support would make the project more sustainable and less of a private burden on my family.

Support helps maintain conditions under which the work can continue honestly.

The problem at the center of this project: how to finish and publish complex creative material without making it smaller just to make it legible.

Support does not buy creative control, private access, or commissioned output.

Supporters will receive occasional updates, process notes, and early looks at selected public-facing material.

Higher tiers may sometimes be invited to suggest themes, questions, or constraints for future experiments, but the work remains independent. Support helps create a more local and attentive weather around the project, instead of leaving it entirely exposed to platform pressure, private strain, and whatever the internet happens to reward.

Now and then

Eventually, the tiers may become stranger, more generous, and more specific as the project finds its rhythm. That is not a promise of scale, but an opening toward future care packages, peculiar multimedia fragments, and small signals worth sending.

I am also open to hearing from authors, editors, designers, developers, and project enablers who recognize the problem at the center of this project:

How do we finish and publish complex creative material without making it smaller just to make it legible?

For now, support helps give the project room to consolidate, continue, and take clearer shape.

Thank you for your consideration.

If you want to support a strange, serious, independent literary project about writing, friendship, voice, memory, and experimental publishing, this Patreon is one way to help.

Thank you for reading.
