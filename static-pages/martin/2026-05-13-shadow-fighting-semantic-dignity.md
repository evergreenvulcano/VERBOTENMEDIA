One genuine tension: The piece argues against reduction while occasionally risking a different kind of reduction—treating "the machine," "the algorithm," "the market" as monolithic forces. You're aware of this (you say "the machine can assist"), but the rhetorical weight of the piece sometimes flattens these systems into undifferentiated capture mechanisms. This might be intentional for Patreon's audience, but it's worth noticing.

Minor note: "Let's remember that always, in every direction, at all times" lands better as a closing than as instruction. It reads less like operating law and more like exhortation. Consider whether that's the register you want at the end.

This will resonate with your readers. It's the kind of piece people share because it names something they already felt but couldn't articulate.
