# Psychotechnical Subjectivity: Working Glossary

- **Section:** Experimental Sandbox
- **Status:** Active

## Summary
An evolving lexicon for naming transformations of selfhood in mediated environments.

## Draft
The term techautist does not appear in any DSM. It is not a diagnosis. It is a cultural portrait — a figure sketched in the Verboten Media working documents to name a condition that the clinical vocabulary cannot reach: a human who possesses full emotional capacity but lacks real potens i det sociala because the social skills their generation developed were optimized for representationernas sociala arena rather than the friction-heavy, boredom-saturated, body-present encounters through which previous generations learned to extend emotion into the interpersonal field.

This glossary does not aim to replace the clinical vocabulary. It aims to supplement it with terms that name what is happening to selfhood in technically mediated environments — terms drawn from the intersection of cognitive science, media theory, literary analysis, and lived experience in the specific historical moment of 2023–2026.

Medierad existens. The condition of having been formed through screens, flows, and content-systems during the developmental period when the self was supposed to consolidate through unmediated encounter. Not a metaphor but a developmental description. The publisher's psyche IS a publishing house because mediation was the only architecture available during construction.

Spegelneuronförlamning. Mirror neuron paralysis. The attenuation of empathic extension that occurs when social interaction is primarily conducted through interfaces that strip away the body's involuntary signals — micro-expressions, postural shifts, breathing rhythms — leaving only text, emoji, and the occasional carefully selected photograph. The mirror neurons are intact. Their inputs have been architecturally reduced.

Skrytsociosfären. The boast-o-sphere. The social media environment understood not as a communication platform but as a competitive display arena in which every post is implicitly an advertisement for the poster's life. The term does not moralize. It describes: this is the water. These are the currents. Here is what you become if you swim here long enough.

Totalteknisk. The condition of having built a total technical system for mediating your own existence and then forgetting that you built it. The publisher's tagline — Tillsammans, tillgivna och totaltekniska — contains the diagnosis inside the branding. The Förlag is totalteknisk: every domain of life has been absorbed into the infrastructure. The question the term forces: is total-technical integration a form of mastery or a form of disappearance?

Utvärderingsmodellen. The five-axis evaluation model developed in the novel for assessing human potential: extroversion-to-concentration ratio, empathy in reasoning, autistic calm, linguistic scaling ability, future-arc visibility. Not a psychometric instrument but a literary instrument — a way of reading people as texts, with axes that no HR department would recognize and no standardized test can measure.

Låtar på hjärnan. Songs on the brain. The theory that certain pieces of music occupy cognitive bandwidth not as entertainment but as operating systems — background processes that shape mood, tempo, and associative patterns below the threshold of conscious attention. The song you cannot get out of your head is not a nuisance. It is running your cognition.

This glossary evolves. Terms are added when existing vocabulary fails to name an observed condition. Terms are revised when the condition they name shifts. No term is permanent. The commitment is not to a fixed lexicon but to the ongoing practice of naming — the belief that what can be named can be examined, and what can be examined can, at minimum, be inhabited with greater precision.


### Praktikantens Arbetsnoteringar
The novel generates its own technical vocabulary — a lexicon of terms that do not exist outside its pages but that, once encountered, become indispensable for describing phenomena the standard vocabulary cannot reach. From Mary: "utvärderingsmodell" (evaluation model, Ch. 9, pp. 87–88), the five-axis diagnostic instrument; "varför inte?" (why not?, Ch. 9), the methodological reversal that transforms inquiry from retrospective to prospective; "riggar" (rigs/cages, Ch. 1), technology as containment structure around the body; "låtar på hjärnan" (songs on the brain, Ch. 9), involuntary earworms as evidence of brain-consciousness separation. From Verboten: "multiplicitetens anda" (spirit of multiplicity, Ch. 8), the organizational commitment to operating at multiple levels simultaneously; "informationalism är en humanism" (Ch. 8), the philosophical claim that information processing is not the opposite of humanism but its most advanced form; "exklusiv selektivism" (Ch. 8), the recruitment principle that exclusion is a form of care. From Arthur: "världens blåaste blå" (world's bluest blue, Ch. 6), the aesthetic absolute; "SHA2-minnen" (SHA2 memories, Ch. 11), consciousness as editable data. From Dawkin: "Allt är kod!" (Everything is code!, Ch. 4), the computational metaphysic. From Skip: "icke-syntetiska medier" (non-synthetic media, Ch. 5), media whose messages cannot be corrupted because they are carved in stone. These terms are not jargon. They are instruments — psychotechnical tools that the novel builds and deploys and leaves for the reader to carry out of the text and into the world.
