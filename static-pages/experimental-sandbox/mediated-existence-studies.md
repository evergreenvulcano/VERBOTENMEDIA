# Mediated Existence Studies

- **Section:** Experimental Sandbox
- **Status:** Active

## Summary
Structured phenomenological observations on daily life in technically saturated environments.

## Draft
"Det ser ut som att du tillber internet," Lillfrugan says — and in seven words she names the condition that this probe attempts to document.

Mediated existence is not a theory. It is Tuesday morning. It is the act of reaching for your phone before your feet touch the floor. It is the specific muscular tension of composing a message you will delete before sending. It is the fourteen seconds spent looking at a photograph of someone you have not spoken to in three years — fourteen seconds that no platform can monetize because they produce no click, no scroll, no engagement signal, only a small private rearrangement of emotional furniture that leaves no trace in any database.

The word medierad carries at least three simultaneous meanings in the Verboten lexicon, and the probe's method is to hold all three without choosing:

Media-ted — formed by screens, flows, content, the apparatus. The 20-year-old entering adulthood today has never experienced an unmediated social environment. Every friendship is partly constituted through platforms. Every romantic encounter is partly filtered through the grammar of the dating app. Every political conviction is partly shaped by the recommendation algorithm that selected which arguments to surface.

Medica-ted — numbed through the developmental years. The period between ten and twenty-seven, when the self is supposed to consolidate through friction, boredom, and unstructured encounter, has been filled instead with content. Not bad content, necessarily. Not harmful content, necessarily. Simply continuous content — a flow that prevents the temporal emptiness in which self-formation occurs.

Mediated — never in direct contact with reality, always through a proxy. The publisher's entire operation is an attempt to de-mediate — to achieve unmediated contact with experience. But the tool chosen for this de-mediation is publishing, which is itself the ultimate mediation technology. He tries to smash the mirror by throwing another mirror at it. This paradox is not a failure. It is the engine.

The probe accumulates observations rather than conclusions. It does not aim to produce a critique of mediated existence — that genre is exhausted, its conclusions predetermined, its audience already convinced. Instead, it operates as phenomenological record: structured notations on what daily life actually feels like inside technically saturated environments, attended to with the same precision a field biologist brings to documenting animal behavior in a habitat.

What does it feel like to see your own face in a video call and adjust your expression for the camera while simultaneously trying to listen to what someone is saying? What does it feel like to receive a notification about a message that turns out to be a notification about a notification? What does it feel like to know, with certainty, that the restaurant you are eating in was selected by an algorithm, and to enjoy the meal anyway, and to feel a small unnameable shame about the enjoyment?

The series does not conclude. It accumulates. Each observation is a data point in a study whose methodology is attention and whose findings are ongoing. The aim is not insight but legibility — making visible the texture of an existence that has become so thoroughly mediated that mediation itself has become invisible, which is, of course, when it is most powerful.

### Praktikantens Arbetsnoteringar
#### Mediation by role
The casino boat Starship Aquarius (Chapter 13) is the novel's laboratory for total mediation. Every interaction aboard is mediated by role (Norman South and Clarence Client dealing blackjack), by game (gambling as formalized social interaction), by performance (Ned Routerman the doorman narrating DMT stories to anyone who will listen), by surveillance (everyone watching everyone), and finally by violence (pirates boarding, Verboten operatives responding). Nothing on the boat is direct. Nothing is unmediated. Even the ocean — vast, indifferent, analog — is experienced through portholes and decks, framed by the boat's architecture. The casino boat is a study in what existence becomes when every possible channel of experience is routed through a mediating structure. Contrast this with Borderland (Chapter 12): "noll procent algoritm," brand-free, participatory, no sponsors, no data capture. The festival stages radically unmediated existence — or attempts to. Bodies encounter bodies. Art is made and consumed in the same gesture. There is no audience because everyone is a participant. And yet the novel complicates even this: the Clone performs poetry at Borderland, which is a mediated act (performer/audience) inside an unmediated space. Mary's PR career (Chapter 7) sits between these poles as professional mediation — she manages the interface between public figures and public perception, mediating Bob Dylan, Zlatan, and hockey crises with the fluency of someone who understands that all public existence is mediated and always has been. The novel does not romanticize unmediated existence. It maps mediation as a spectrum from total (casino boat) to minimal (Borderland) and asks: where on the spectrum is life most livable?
