# Cultural Diagnostics: A Survey

- **Section:** Experimental Sandbox
- **Status:** Probe · Scheduled

## Summary
A structured characterization of the present cultural moment using empirical anchors.

## Draft
Mary builds a five-axis evaluation model for assessing human beings. The axes: extroversion-to-concentration ratio, empathy in reasoning, autistic calm, linguistic scaling ability, future-arc visibility. No HR department would recognize these categories. No standardized test can measure them. They are literary axes — instruments for reading people as texts, developed inside a novel that treats assessment as a creative act rather than an administrative procedure.

This probe attempts something analogous at the cultural scale: a structured characterization of the current moment that refuses both optimism and decline narratives, using specific empirical anchors rather than impressionistic mood-reading.

The conventional approaches fail because they are captured by their own genres. The "everything is terrible" narrative (surveillance capitalism, attention crisis, democratic erosion, ecological collapse) produces accurate observations inside a framework so pessimistic that it paralyzes action. The "everything is fine, actually" narrative (global poverty reduction, medical advances, expanding literacy, solar energy curves) produces accurate observations inside a framework so optimistic that it obscures the specific mechanisms through which harm operates. Neither is diagnostic. Both are performative — they are moods disguised as analyses.

A genuine diagnostic requires axes. Not one axis (good/bad, progress/decline, hope/despair) but multiple axes that can move independently, producing a characterization with internal contradictions that resist narrative smoothing. The novel's five-axis model provides a structural template:

Axis 1: Medierad vs. oförmedlad. The ratio of mediated to unmediated experience in daily life — not as a moral judgment but as a measurable parameter. How many hours of the day involve direct sensory contact with the physical environment vs. contact mediated through screens? The number itself is neutral. The trend is diagnostic.

Axis 2: Concentration capacity under algorithmic pressure. Not attention span in the abstract but the specific cognitive ability to sustain focus when the technical environment is architecturally designed to interrupt. This is not a personal virtue. It is a structural condition. The person who cannot concentrate in 2026 is not weaker than the person who concentrated in 1996. They are operating inside a different attentional infrastructure.

Axis 3: Tribal vs. atomized social form. The degree to which social bonds are organized through genuine mutual obligation (the Förlag's "tribal mapping") vs. platform-mediated loose association (the follower, the connection, the "friend"). Both forms exist simultaneously. The diagnostic measures their ratio and its direction.

Axis 4: Linguistic compression. The capacity of available language — public discourse, institutional communication, interpersonal exchange — to carry complex meaning. When language compresses (tweets, captions, subject lines), certain kinds of thought become structurally impossible. When language expands (essays, novels, long-form dialogue), other kinds of thought become possible but find no audience. The diagnostic measures compression as a cultural parameter.

Axis 5: Future-arc visibility. The capacity of the present cultural configuration to generate credible images of the future. Not predictions — images. The novel's children who cannot grow up are a diagnostic of future-arc collapse: a generation that cannot imagine adulthood because the available models of adulthood have been evacuated of content. The diagnostic asks: what futures are currently imaginable, by whom, and through what cultural infrastructure?

The survey administers these axes to the present. The results are contradictory. That is the point.


### Praktikantens Arbetsnoteringar
#### Private and Extrememly Subjective Axial Social Matrix
Mary's utvärderingsmodell (Chapter 9, pp. 87–88) is the novel's primary diagnostic instrument: five axes for reading social interactions at the individual level. It is a tool built by a teenager who needed to understand adults and found that intuition was insufficient — so she built a system. The system works. It produces readings. It is, in the strictest sense, a cultural diagnostic operated at the scale of a single conversation. Skip's gravestone thesis (Chapter 5, pp. 44–46) scales the diagnostic upward: gravestones as media that diagnose trustworthiness across centuries. A gravestone's message cannot be edited, hacked, or algorithmically promoted. It sits in a field and says what it says for as long as the stone endures. Skip's argument — that "budskapen skulle kunna korrumperas" (the messages could be corrupted) in synthetic media but not in stone — is a diagnostic of media integrity at civilizational scale. Borderland festival (Chapter 12) operates the diagnostic at the cultural level: what does culture look like at zero algorithmic interference? What art gets made? What conversations happen? What falls apart? The festival is a controlled experiment in cultural self-organization, and its results — chaotic, beautiful, sometimes fatal (someone dies in the hospital tent) — constitute a diagnostic that no survey or study could replicate. And Arthur's trial (Chapter 16) is the juridical-cultural diagnostic: the legal system attempting to evaluate whether destroying a color constitutes "allmänfarlig ödeläggelse" (public endangerment). The trial is a diagnostic that cannot compute its own question. The legal system has categories for property destruction, environmental damage, public safety — but it has no category for chromatic annihilation. Four diagnostic systems, four scales (individual, civilizational, cultural, juridical), one novel that deploys them all without privileging any.
