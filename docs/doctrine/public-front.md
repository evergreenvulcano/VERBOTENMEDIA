# Public Front Doctrine

Status: canonical doctrine for current public-front build work.
Readiness: provisional.

Note: This doctrine is accepted as a working northstar, but it may need to be rebuilt
when the difference is clearer between repo-internal rebuild rhetoric and the repo's
final collaborator-facing usefulness.

## What Public Front Is Not

### Not Website

A website presents something finished to the public. A public front exposes
something under maturation so it can be tested. A website says welcome. A public
front says: here is the process.

### Not Portfolio

A portfolio archives finished work. A public front is infrastructure for work in
progress. It asks what is becoming possible here, not what can be showcased.

### Not Dashboard

A dashboard is a control panel for a service. A public front is a passage apparatus
for a work. Dashboards are for users and management. Public fronts are for
collaborators and witnesses.

### Not Product

A product is consumed, bought, used, or adopted. A public front can be read from
and tested against. It seeks tension rather than usage.

### Not Second Brain

A second brain stores thoughts. A public front shows thought under maturation. It
does not privatize memory; it makes relations testable.

### Not Community

Community is where people gather. A public front is where a work exposes its work.
It allows witness and bounded collaboration without becoming social infrastructure.

## What Public Front Is

A public front is a public passage apparatus where a work can:

1. Expose method without reducing it to content.
2. Let external actors test ideas without absorbing them as features or ownership.
3. Present status through ceremonial acts rather than progress tracking.
4. Keep people at the right distance: not too close as community, not too far as a
   polished brand surface.
5. Refuse some explanations so the reader must work to understand.

## Why It Matters

Public work often becomes either too closed or too open.

Too closed: portfolio, website, brand. The work cannot move.

Too open: community, channels, feedback loops. The work is absorbed by its public.

A public front is a third path: open enough to test ideas before others, closed
enough for the work to remain its own.

## Principles

### 1. Read Without Consuming

The reader may read method, Banketter, and experiment. Reading is not consumption.
The reader becomes witness, not customer or user.

### 2. Exposure With Concrete Exceptions

The apparatus and selected source-bearing artifacts may be public. Presence in
the leading public branch establishes a publication presumption. A source body
remains withheld only when a concrete exception is named: private or personal
data, credentials, local machine paths, unclear rights, or explicit human
withholding.

### 3. Synthesis Without Reduction

Banketter synthesize work from a period, but they are ceremonial rather than
summaries. They present what is now possible, not a progress digest.

### 4. Collaboration Without Ownership

An external idea may be developed by the apparatus. That does not grant the sender
ownership over the result.

### 5. Contribution Without Steering

Criticism may matter. It may affect direction. It does not become governance.

### 6. Status Without Measurement

Banketter give status without metrics, percentages, completion claims, or product
milestones.

### 7. Future Without Design Lock

A Bankett may open future possibilities without promising next steps.

### 8. Bounded Destabilization

The public front may publish fragmentary artifacts, research specimens, and
process bodies that destabilize the repository's existing categories.

This is allowed when the instability is the point of the publication and its
status is explicit. A specimen must not impersonate settled doctrine,
canonical storyworld, finished literature, or neutral research. Its
provenance conditions, mediation, incompletion, and capacity for later
revision must remain legible.

The repository is not only permitted to represent what it already is. It may
use publication as a vector of becoming.

## Publication Consequence

It is acceptable to publish something unfinished if it is methodologically clear.
It is acceptable to refuse overexplanation. It is acceptable to say that some
entrances are not for everyone. It is not acceptable to expose material covered
by a concrete exception or convert the work into service logic.

Explicit human authorization may open a named boundary for a bounded specimen
publication. The leading public branch now carries a general publication
presumption; future exceptions must be named rather than inferred from older
status anxiety.
