# Psychotechnical Subjectivity: A Field Note

- **Section:** Writings
- **Type:** Analysis
- **Year:** 2023

## Summary
A preliminary mapping of the term across cognitive science, media theory, and lived platform experience.

## Draft
The term does not appear in any standard glossary. It is a compound built from pressure — *psycho-* (the interior life of the mind, its drives and architectures) welded to *-technical* (the systems, interfaces, and infrastructures through which that interior life is increasingly mediated and shaped). Psychotechnical subjectivity names the condition of being a self whose self-formation occurs substantially through technical apparatus, and who may not be able to distinguish between the self that does the forming and the tools through which the forming happens.

The Verboten Media working documents sketch a figure called *techautisten* — not a diagnosis but a cultural portrait. The techautist is a human who lacks *real potens i det sociala* — real potency in the social. Not because of pathology, but because the social skills that previous generations developed through unmediated, friction-heavy, boredom-saturated interaction have been replaced by a different skill set: one optimized for *representationernas sociala arena* — the social arena of representations. The techautist can navigate a group chat with surgical precision but freezes in a room where bodies are present and reactions arrive at the speed of facial expression rather than the speed of typing.

This is not a generational insult. It is a structural observation. The millennial who came of age on forums and early social media developed one psychotechnical profile. The Gen Z native who has never known a world without algorithmic curation developed another. Neither is superior. Both are *shaped* — formed through sustained interaction with technical systems that reward certain cognitive patterns and attenuate others.

The field note maps three axes along which psychotechnical subjectivity operates:

*Cognitive architecture.* How do technical systems reshape the way attention, memory, and judgment function? The 147,000-character Verboten fragment analysis documents how recommendation algorithms exploit specific neurological vulnerabilities — prefrontal cortex immaturity, mirror neuron system manipulation, dopamine pathway hijacking — to install what it calls *market logic as interpretive framework*. Children's cognitive development becomes optimized for market participation rather than critical thinking. This is not an accidental side effect. It is a design outcome.

*Affective calibration.* The techautist possesses emotions — *den är en människa* — but lacks the componentry that enables their extension into the social domain. Mirror neurons can be awakened, the documents suggest, through *medveten koncentrerad träning i att delta i andra människors sinne* — conscious concentrated training in participating in other people's minds. Reading fiction is named as one method. The irony is precise: a technical problem (psychotechnical attenuation) requires a pre-technical solution (narrative empathy).

*Identity formation.* Platform algorithms identify what children want to become and then monetize the pathway. Aspiration mapping, peer pressure simulation, future-self manipulation — the mechanisms are documented, specific, and operational. The result is a subjectivity that experiences itself as autonomous while being shaped by systems whose optimization targets are engagement metrics, not human flourishing.

Psychotechnical subjectivity is not a problem to be solved. It is a *condition to be diagnosed* — mapped, named, made legible — so that the humans living inside it can begin to distinguish between what they chose and what was chosen for them.

### Praktikantens Arbetsnoteringar
#### "Psychotechnical Subjects" 
The novel deploys at least three distinct psychotechnical frameworks, each operated by a different character, each treating human subjectivity as something that can be systematically read, measured, and — crucially — acted upon. Mary's five-axis evaluation model (Chapter 9, pp. 87–88) is the most explicit: a diagnostic instrument for reading social interactions in real-time, developed by a fourteen-year-old who needed a system because intuition alone was not reliable. The axes are precise (extroversion/concentration ratio, empathy in reasoning, autistic calm, linguistic scaling ability, future-arc visibility) and operational — they produce actionable readings, not theoretical descriptions. Then there is Mary's theory of "låtar på hjärnan" — songs on the brain (Chapter 9, pp. 92–93). Involuntary earworms, she argues, are evidence that consciousness and brain are separate systems: the brain plays a song that consciousness did not request, suggesting that the brain has its own agenda, its own psychotechnical operations, running beneath or beside the conscious self. It is a folk-phenomenological argument, but the novel treats it with the same seriousness as any philosophical claim. Parallel to these, Dawkin's code-as-religion obsession — "Allt är kod!" (Everything is code!) — reads Kierkegaard as proto-coder, absorbs Nick Bostrom's simulation argument (Chapters 4–5, pp. 27–51), and produces a psychotechnical framework where subjectivity itself is computational, where the self is a program that can, in principle, be debugged. Three frameworks, one novel, zero reconciliation. The novel does not choose between them. It lets them run concurrently, like parallel processes on a server that never quite crashes.
