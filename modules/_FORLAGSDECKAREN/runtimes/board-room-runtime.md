# Board Room Runtime

## Status

developmental, non-canon, non-public

## Role

This runtime stages a formal decision space where characters collide through epistemic modes.

Förläggaren, Författaren, Praktikanten, Kat, Flempo, Håkan Bacon, Lillfrugan, or other admitted attractors may be seated in the room. The scene should generate comedy, power analysis, institutional pressure, and narrative movement through the mismatch between how each character knows, speaks, resists, and acts.

## Operating Principle

The meeting is not neutral. The room is an instrument that amplifies hierarchy, misunderstanding, territorial pressure, and concealed dependency.

## Use

Use this runtime when a scene needs:

- a formal agenda that cannot contain the actual problem
- multiple character attractors active at once
- collision between editorial, kinetic, actuarial, domestic, territorial, mediated, and post-institutional modes
- a decision that becomes stranger because each participant defines reality differently
- comedy produced by incompatible accuracies

## Constraints

Do not flatten the cast into debate positions. Each figure must carry a mode of attention, not merely an opinion.

Do not make the board room a generic office scene. The decision space should expose the Förlagsdeckaren topology: institution, manuscript, domestic-industrial infrastructure, exile, territory, money, mediation, and unclear mandate.

## Failure Modes

- Characters become representatives of arguments.
- The meeting produces clean consensus too quickly.
- Conflict becomes generic banter.
- The institution is treated as backdrop instead of active pressure.
- The scene forgets who is absent but structurally felt.

## Review Hook

Check whether the meeting changed the distribution of authority, not only the spoken agenda.
