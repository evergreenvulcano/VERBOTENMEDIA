# Tankefigur Method

Tankefigur / Thought-Figure is ontological design as literary-method architecture: a radically subjective fiction that produces generative output. It is specific enough to take risks and portable enough to be used by others without becoming a generic design-thinking exercise.

The method begins with a figure held inside a square tent: fragile shell and adventurous character at once. That square sits inside other squares, membrane on membrane. The layers relate to one another while remaining sufficient in themselves.

At the square-corners, words become world-constituting coordinates. The corners are not decoration; they are anchors for the figure's reality field. The square-stream pulses outward like a mental wavelength, ends, returns, and behaves like a portal or transitional phase.

The model-becomes-substrate principle is central: one model becomes the ground for a further generative model. A wheel of feelings can become a reference bank. That bank can be patched into signal synthesis, character logic, scene construction, interface design, or review language.

Parody belongs here when it is made with love. The parody/love logic produces a character-gallery: iconic tropes, metadata, references, and usable masks. The gallery is not merely comic inventory; it is a way to let figures carry cultural pressure without flattening them into slogans.

The martyr-complex countermove separates being a victim from making a sacrifice. The method can hold wounds, refusal, and pressure, but it should not reward passive sanctification. Cost has to be read structurally.

The author-role is treated as a social function: a channel into collective consciousness for anyone who wants to plant something there. Authorship is not reduced to biography; it becomes a role, a surface, a relay.

Hyperindividualism appears as a concrete art project during coming-of-age. The radical personal charge is not the endpoint. It is material that can become collective, transferable, and operational.

The method refuses the simple hero's journey. Individual ascent does not settle the work. The ground of the final product must be vast in quantity and precision, and it must resist revenge, self-congratulation, and easy redemption.

The distributed cost principle follows: the whole is borne by more than one figure. Any runtime using Tankefigur must account for the surrounding bodies, interfaces, maintenance loops, and relational load that make the work possible.
