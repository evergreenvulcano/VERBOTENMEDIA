# Alignment Signals

## Purpose

Alignment signals are observable indicators for weekly review by a human or supporting agent. They test whether action, attention, output, and relationships remain aligned with the module's declared intent.

## Positive Signals

### Execution

Planned work completes without dependence on panic, guilt, or last-minute urgency.

### Prioritization

Priorities remain stable from week to week. Refusal remains possible without prolonged negotiation.

### Integrity

Cognitive dissonance stays low. Rationalizations decrease. Course correction happens quickly when drift is detected.

### Attention

Attention remains purpose-bound, with fewer compulsive checks and more sustained focus.

### Relationships

Presence and reliability increase. Commitments are kept.

### Learning

Synthesis changes decisions rather than merely accumulating stored knowledge.

### Maintenance Loops

Energy and throughput remain steady across cycles. Recovery functions persist under load.

### Creative Output

Artifacts ship as coherent minima and improve through iteration.

## Relationship-Protective Defaults

Time is proactively blocked for close interfaces. Device-mediated half-presence is treated as a fault condition, not a neutral default.

## Integrity Under Pressure

When incentives conflict, actions remain plainly explainable without rationalization.

## Intentional Screen Use

Screens open with a stated purpose and close when that purpose is met.

## Placeholders

- Domain-specific indicators: add project-level measures as the module is used, such as publishing cadence, review quality, runtime usefulness, or audience feedback.
- Drift-prevention signals: add early warning indicators, friction checks, and recovery triggers when repeated drift patterns become visible.
