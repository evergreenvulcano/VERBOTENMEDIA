**PART I**

**KARAVANEN**

*A mobile server hall container, branded SPRÄCK INDUSTRIES DATAHALL 07, hitched to the Booleanska Cirkusen convoy. Parked somewhere between cities. The GPS says Karlstad. The body says Sundsvall. Both are lying, or both are correct, which amounts to the same thing when you have been awake for twenty-one hours and the distinction between geography and autobiography has dissolved.*

The hour is past two. Not two in the morning — that implies a relationship between the number and the phenomenon that has ceased to operate. It is the kind of late where the hour stops being information and becomes weather. A climate of wakefulness. An atmosphere of the kind of tiredness that does not want sleep but wants something to look at, something to hold in the hands, something to read with the particular attention that only arrives after the responsible brain has logged off and left the night shift to whatever remains.

The caravan hums. Twenty-seven cooling fans distributed across six rack units, each producing a slightly different frequency, together forming a chord that Jens Spräck has learned to hear as silence. When the chord changes — when a fan speeds up or slows down — that is noise. The chord itself is the absence of noise. The infrastructure's resting heartbeat.

Mixed into the chord: residual circus sounds. The Booleanska Cirkusen does not sleep cleanly. There is a generator running two trailers over — the backup unit for the main tent's lighting rig, which runs all night because someone once forgot to turn it off and the electrician, whose name is either Göran or Jörgen (Spräck has never successfully retained which), declared it would damage the capacitors to power-cycle it, a claim that may or may not be true but has become institutional doctrine. There is a guy wire creaking — a rhythmic, metallic complaint from the equipment trailer's tie-down, expanding and contracting with the temperature differential between the ground and the night air. And there is something animal two trailers over. Not identifiable by species. Something between a breath and a grunt. The circus keeps animals — not performing animals, retired ones, animals from circuses that have closed, a kind of pension system for creatures whose professional lives have ended but whose biological lives continue. Spräck respects this arrangement. He has never said so. He respects it the way he respects all systems that operate without requiring his opinion.

The server racks cast blue-green light across the interior of the container. LED status indicators: forty-seven green, eleven amber, two red. The red ones have been red for six weeks. Nobody has filed a ticket. The system they monitored was decommissioned in March but the rack unit was never pulled. It sits in its bay like a pensioned circus animal — no longer performing but still breathing, still blinking its two red eyes at the ceiling.

His desk is a fold-out shelf between rack units four and five. The shelf was designed to hold a keyboard and a diagnostic monitor. It holds, currently: a laptop (open, screen dark, waiting), a mug that says DATA ÄR DATA in a font that was chosen by a committee and looks like it, a half-eaten pizza in a branded box, and his phone, face down, notifications disabled since 21:00.

The pizza box says PIZZA FRÅN FRAMTIDEN. The Spräck Industries branded pizza program. It was meant to be a joke. An internal gag at a team retreat in 2023 — someone mocked up a pizza box with the Spräck Industries logo and the slogan and everyone laughed and then someone made fifty of them and then someone partnered with a local pizzeria and then the pizzeria became two pizzerias and then the boxes started appearing at events and then a regional newspaper wrote about it and then it became a distribution channel. Not for pizza. For brand presence. The pizza was incidental. The box was the product. The box that traveled from kitchen to table to recycling bin, carrying the logo through the domestic infrastructure of Swedish life. Every pizza consumed was a brand impression completed.

Spräck ate three slices at 22:30. The pizza was adequate. The pizza is always adequate. The pizza is not the point.

*He has been awake since 05:30. The day has been full. Everything he does is full. He gave a demo at 10:00 — mossbox prototype v1.2 for a regional delegation from Värmland, seven people in fleece vests who nodded at the right moments and asked questions that revealed they had read the briefing document, which is unusual and which Spräck noted with the particular appreciation he reserves for people who prepare. He did a podcast interview at 14:00, forty-five minutes on mobile data infrastructure and circus economics, during which he produced the sentence "Data är data, ekonomin växer, planeten består" and immediately recognized it as a pull quote, the kind of sentence that would be extracted from the conversation and placed in a graphic and circulated independently of its context, which is what pull quotes do, which is what he does: generates extractable units of meaning that circulate without their source and gain meaning from the circulation rather than from the original utterance.*

He watched the evening circus performance from the tech booth. He was supposed to be running diagnostics on the rack cooling system. He watched the trapeze instead. The trapeze artist — her name is Maja, she is twenty-three, she has been with the Booleanska Cirkusen for two seasons — performed a sequence that involved releasing the bar at the apex of the swing, turning twice in the air, and catching the return bar with a grip that looked casual and was not. Spräck watched this from behind a diagnostic terminal and thought about release timing. About the moment you let go. About the relationship between the height of the arc and the precision of the catch. He did not complete the cooling diagnostics.

And now it is past two. And he cannot sleep. Not insomnia — that implies a dysfunction. This is the conversion. The point where fatigue stops requesting sleep and starts requesting attention. The body gives up on rest and activates sonar. The Dujag starts. He did not start it. He never starts it. The Dujag is not a program he runs. It is a mode the system enters when the conditions are met: fatigue plus solitude plus proximity to material. The radical empathy protocol. The cognitive mode where reading stops being comprehension and becomes habitation.

He opens the laptop. The screen wakes. The pipeline dashboard loads.

**PART II**

**SYNK**

The dashboard is not beautiful. It was designed by someone who understood data and did not understand what data looks like when it is being looked at by a human at two in the morning in a server caravan. It is functional. Gray background. Blue data points. Orange alert indicators. Three panels: PIPELINE TRAFFIC (72h), SOURCE CLUSTER ANALYSIS, DISTRIBUTION NODE STATUS.

He reads the pipeline traffic panel first. Force of habit. The same way he reads a room when he enters it — perimeter first, center second, exits third. The traffic numbers cover the last seventy-two hours of the Spräck Industries / Verboten Media content ecosystem. Not server metrics. Not uptime, latency, packet loss. Media metrics. Mentions. Articles. Social circulation. Format distribution. Temperature readings.

The numbers are higher than expected.

He leans forward. Not dramatically — this is not a film — but with the particular two-centimeter forward shift that indicates the difference between scanning and reading, between the body's autopilot and the body's engagement. Two centimeters. The distance between leisure and attention.

Something happened in the last seventy-two hours that he did not initiate. This is the detail that catches. He knows the normal rhythm of his own media circulation — the baseline hum, the expected spikes around scheduled events, the decay curves after press releases, the low background radiation of ongoing mentions that never fully stops because the pipeline never fully stops. This is above baseline. Significantly.

The amplification curve shows the characteristic sigmoid: slow start at the base, the hesitant first mentions, then the steep middle section where the mentions start citing each other and the circulation becomes self-referential and the curve bends upward with the particular acceleration that indicates the system has ingested the trigger and is now processing it through all available channels. Plateau approaching. The top of the S-curve, where the velocity of new mentions slows because the available audience has been saturated and the only new territory is reinterpretation — the same event being redescribed in different formats for different audiences, which generates volume but not reach.

He recognizes this curve. He designed the conditions for this curve. Not this specific instance — he did not trigger this particular amplification event — but the architecture. The pipeline architecture that converts a single input into a multi-format, multi-channel, self-amplifying distribution cascade. The contagion as the product. Not the content as the product. The contagion.

He taught this. He has stood on stages and drawn this curve on whiteboards and said: "Produkten är inte det ni säljer. Produkten är det som händer efter." The product is not what you sell. The product is what happens after. The after is the contagion. The spread. The reformatting. The moment the original event stops being an event and becomes a format — a template that other formats use to generate their own content, which generates more formats, which generates more content, which is the pipeline, which is the product.

But now the contagion is about him.

He sits with this. The system he designed to amplify is amplifying him. The architect inside his own building. The epidemiologist who catches his own virus. The analogy breaks down because analogy always breaks down — the thing itself is more precise than any comparison. He is not like an architect inside his own building. He IS the architect inside his own building. There is no like. There is only the is.

He clicks through to the source cluster. The panel reorganizes. Four source documents emerge from the traffic data — four formats published in the last seventy-two hours, each covering the same event. A product launch? A statement? A municipal collaboration? He cannot immediately tell. The formats have already done their work. The event has been reformatted four times and each format has claimed it as its own and the original event — whatever it was — exists now only as the intersection of four different descriptions, none of which agree on what they are describing.

He reads. The Dujag calibrates. He leans toward the screen.

**PART III**

**FYRA FORMAT**

Four formats. Four blindnesses. He reads them in order, which is not the order they were published but the order the dashboard presents them, which is by amplification volume, highest first, which means he reads the warmest take first and the coldest last, which is the wrong order for understanding but the right order for impact, which is the dashboard's priority, which is the pipeline's priority, which is his priority, which is the problem.

**FORMAT 1: LOKAL KOLUMN — Kommunnytt**

"Invigning med framtidstro — Spräck ska göra skillnad för hela kommunen."

Warm. Supportive. The municipal newspaper doing what the municipal newspaper does: converting events into affirmation, converting complexity into pride, converting the unknown into the known quantity of community spirit. The article is 400 words of radiant optimism. Hans Holm, kommunalråd, is quoted: "Vi är stolta över att kunna erbjuda denna möjlighet till våra medborgare." Lena Nord, verksamhetschef, is quoted: "Det digitala dialogverktyget ger oss helt nya möjligheter." Mats Ali, projektledare, is quoted: "Samarbetet med Spräck Industries har varit inspirerande och professionellt."

The fakta box in the margin: "Digitalt dialogverktyg. Lanserat: [datum]. Finansiering: Kommunal budget med stöd av regionala utvecklingsmedel. Syfte: Öka medborgarnas delaktighet i kommunala beslut."

Spräck reads this and recognizes the format's blindness immediately. It has turned him into a municipal asset. A thing that makes the kommun feel modern. A vendor of futurity. The kommun believes it hired him. It believes it issued a procurement, evaluated tenders, selected a supplier, signed a contract. The kommun always thinks it hired him. Every kommun thinks it hired him.

He always thinks the kommun is the laboratory.

This is not cynicism. He does not exploit municipalities. He serves them. The service is real. The dialogverktyg works. Citizens use it. Participation increases. Decisions improve. The metrics are genuine. But the metrics measure the product, and the product is not the product. The product is the format — the story of a municipality that modernized, the case study that travels to the next municipality, the narrative contagion that converts one procurement into five, five into twelve, twelve into a regional standard. The kommun hired him to build a tool. He used the hiring to build a story. The story builds the next hiring. The pipeline.

He has been this before. He recognizes himself in the format the way you recognize yourself in a photograph taken from an angle you do not use for mirrors — correct but unfamiliar. Yes, that is his ear. Yes, that is the back of his head. Yes, that is Jens Spräck as seen by a municipality that needed him to be a solution.

**FORMAT 2: NATIONELL KRÖNIKA — Riksdagen/Kultur**

"Spräck: symbolen för dålig samtid — inte dess lösning."

Cold. Analytical. The critical distance of the national commentariat, which views everything from the capital and mistakes the view for objectivity. The krönikör — unsigned, which is its own statement — has constructed a timeline:

10:02 Invigning. Spräck klipper bandet. (There was a band. There is always a band. The band is the format's entrance fee — the physical ritual that the camera requires.)
 11:03 Första test. Live demonstration for media. (He remembers this. The demonstration worked. It always works during the demonstration.)
 12:17 Reaktion i sociala medier. First mentions, first shares, first commentary. (The sigmoid begins.)
 14:10 Kommunens pressmeddelande. The official version. (The kommun's version, which is not the event but the kommun's reading of the event.)

"Vilka hade påverkat processen? Inga svar i underlaget."

Spräck reads this and nods. Not performatively — there is no one to perform for. He nods because the diagnosis is correct. The krönikör has identified the gap: the process is opaque. The decision chain that led from "idea" to "funded project" to "public launch" is not documented in any publicly available format. The transparency that the system promises is not applied to the system itself. The dialogverktyg enables citizen participation in municipal decisions but the decision to deploy the dialogverktyg was not subject to citizen participation. The democracy tool was deployed undemocratically. This is true. The krönikör is correct.

Correct diagnosis. Wrong patient.

The krönikör thinks the question is about transparency. The question is about what the transparency would reveal if anyone actually looked. Not corruption — Spräck does not do corruption, not because he is ethical (he is, but that is not the reason) but because corruption is inefficient, corruption requires secrecy, and secrecy is the opposite of the pipeline. What the transparency would reveal is something more disorienting than corruption: it would reveal that the process worked exactly as designed, that the design was open, that the decisions were made in meetings with minutes, that the minutes are available on request, and that none of this answers the krönikör's actual question, which is not "what happened" but "why does this feel like something is being hidden." The answer to that question is: because the system generates the feeling of hiddenness as a byproduct of its visibility. The more you show, the more people suspect you are not showing. The transparency is the source of the opacity. This is not a bug.

**FORMAT 3: SEKTORSPALT — Digital förvaltning**

"Case: Spräck — från dialog till datadriven styrsignal."

The trade press. The format that reads everything as a case study. The format that exists to convert events into transferable knowledge, which means converting lived experience into KPIs, which means converting the unmeasurable into the measured, which means losing the thing you were trying to capture.

KPIs: 2,846 användare. 73% engagemang. 18 min genomsnittlig sessionstid. Benchmark targets met or exceeded. Process flow diagram: Insamling → Klassificering → Analys → Återkoppling. Four boxes connected by arrows. The arrows imply sequence. The sequence implies causation. The causation implies control. None of this is entirely false but all of it is incomplete in the specific way that makes incompleteness indistinguishable from accuracy.

Spräck reads the KPIs and has the particular Spräck response: the numbers are correct and they measure the wrong thing.

2,846 användare. Yes. Twenty-eight hundred and forty-six people used the tool. The tool counted them. The tool is good at counting the people who use the tool. What the tool cannot count — what no tool can count — is the people who were changed by proximity to the engagement without engaging themselves. The spouse who heard about it at the dinner table. The colleague who saw the notification but did not click. The neighbor who noticed the sign outside the municipal office and thought something — something unrecorded, something that shifted their relationship to the municipality by a fraction of a degree, a shift that will not show up in any KPI but will show up in the next election, or in the tone of a conversation at a bus stop, or in the decision to attend or not attend a public meeting three months from now.

The thirty percent outside the frame. The nog.

73% engagemang. The engagement metric captures the people who engaged. It cannot capture the quality of the engagement, which is not a metric, which is a texture, which is the difference between clicking because you care and clicking because you were asked to click. The metric treats all clicks as equal. The metric is a democracy of clicks. In the democracy of clicks, the passionate citizen and the bored employee and the curious teenager and the dutiful spouse all register identically: one engagement. One data point. One unit of democratic participation, measured and recorded and presented in a bar chart in a trade magazine, where it will be read by a municipal digitalization officer in another municipality who will use it to justify the procurement of the same tool, which will produce similar numbers, which will appear in another trade magazine, which is the pipeline.

He does not write anything in the notes app. The observation is too familiar to record. He has thought this thought before. He has thought this thought so many times that it has become infrastructure — a permanent cognitive installation that he no longer notices, the way he no longer notices the server hum.

**FORMAT 4: BILAGEKRÖNIKA — Helg/Bilaga**

"Spräck — en skönhet i tidens spricka."

The weekend supplement. The format that has permission to feel. The format that exists in the space between news and literature, between journalism and essay, between description and longing. The bilagekrönikör writes about Spräck the way one writes about a landscape — not as a subject to be analyzed but as a phenomenon to be experienced.

"Ett samtalsobjekt, en spegel av vår längtan att bli hörda."

A conversation object. A mirror of our longing to be heard. Spräck reads this and the Dujag activates differently — not the analytical register that processed the first three formats but the physical one. The somatic register. The one that does not produce thoughts but produces sensations. Something in the chest. Not emotion exactly — more like weather. An internal barometric shift.

"I vår ängsliga tid växer till och med misstaget för att skapa hela med varandet."

He reads this sentence three times. Three full passes, each slower than the last, each producing a different layer of comprehension. First pass: the words. Second pass: the syntax, the way "misstaget" and "hela" and "varandet" are positioned relative to each other, the grammar of imperfection-as-creation. Third pass: the question he cannot answer — is this sentence about him, or is it about the format that wrote it? Is the krönikör describing Spräck, or is the krönikör describing the krönikör's own desire to find beauty in breakage? Is he the crack in time, or is the crack in time the space where the format positions itself in order to feel something?

He cannot decide. The indecision is not a failure of comprehension. The indecision is the comprehension.

He stands up. The caravan ceiling forces him to hunch slightly. He takes one step toward the door, changes his mind, sits back down.

*"Fyra format. Fyra blindheter. Och alla fyra vet exakt vem jag är — förutom den jag som sitter här."*

He says this aloud. To the server racks. To the amber LEDs. To the two red lights on the decommissioned unit. To the pizza box. To nobody.

Samma händelse. Fyra format. Who sees the function?

The Dujag answers: nobody. The function is invisible to every format because the function IS the generation of formats. You cannot see the generator from inside the generated. The engine is invisible from the vehicle. The pipeline is invisible from the content that flows through it. The architect is invisible from inside the building.

He is inside his own building. All four walls are mirrors. Each mirror shows a different reflection. None of the reflections is wrong. None is complete.

**PART IV**

**OFFENTLIG TEMPERATUR**

The pipeline artifact loads. The artefakt-till-offentlig-temperatur diagram. A flow chart — boxes and arrows, the universal language of systems thinking, the format that claims to make complexity visible and in doing so replaces the complexity with a drawing of the complexity, which is not the same thing but is more shareable, which is why it exists, which is the pipeline.

He traces the process with a finger on the screen. Literally. His index finger on the laptop display, following the arrows the way a child follows a river on a map. The Dujag has regressed him — not to childhood but to the kind of attention that childhood produces: direct, physical, unmediated by irony.

Input: "Jens Spräck lanserar mackapären / moss-boxen." Category: Picknickpryl / Analog applikation. Purpose: "Fångar solens energi. Kan även påverka verklighet." Risk: low.

He reads the input specification and the risk assessment and makes a sound — not a laugh, something shorter, something between an exhale and an acknowledgment. Risk: low. The moss-box, assessed for risk by a system that measures risk as the probability of negative media coverage. The probability is low because the product is absurd enough to be charming and functional enough to be defensible. A moss-covered box that captures solar energy and "kan även påverka verklighet" — can also affect reality. The qualifier "även" doing the work of an entire philosophy department. Also. In addition. As a secondary function. The box's primary function is to capture solar energy. Its secondary function is to affect reality. The product roadmap of a man who considers affecting reality a stretch goal.

Transformation. He traces the arrows:

Amplification → Circulation → Reinterpretation → Crisis meeting → Leak → Public temperature rising.

Six stages. Each arrow is a conversion — the event changing state, the way water changes state, from solid to liquid to gas, from local product launch to national talking point to institutional crisis. Not because the product is dangerous. Not because the launch failed. Not because anything went wrong. Because the pipeline processes inputs and the processing generates heat and the heat is the temperature and the temperature is the output. The pipeline does not distinguish between a genuinely dangerous product and a moss-covered picnic accessory. The pipeline amplifies. The amplification generates temperature. The temperature generates response. The response generates more amplification. The loop.

Output. He reads the outputs listed at the bottom of the diagram: Instagram posts. Local newspaper headlines. ("Nationell kris efter Spräcks utrullning.") Internal memos. Product sheets. Gossip bubbles. Municipal investigation chaos.

Nationell kris efter Spräcks utrullning. National crisis after Spräck's rollout. A moss-box. A thing that sits on a windowsill and collects sunlight and contains actual moss. This triggered a national crisis narrative. Not because the nation was in crisis but because the pipeline converts everything into crisis because crisis is the temperature setting that maximizes circulation, and circulation is the product, and the product is the product.

He recognizes the pattern because he designed it. Or rather: he designed the conditions for this pattern to emerge, which is different from designing it but produces the same outcomes and the same deniability. The distinction between "I designed this" and "I designed the conditions from which this emerged" is the distinction between an engineer and an architect, between a programmer and an ecosystems thinker, between someone who builds machines and someone who builds environments in which machines build themselves. He is the second kind. The environments kind. The conditions kind.

He knows the temperature is a feature, not a bug. He also knows that saying this publicly would increase the temperature, which would prove his point, which would increase the temperature further. The self-proving thesis. The snake that eats its own tail and calls the meal a business plan.

The Dujag makes him feel the feedback loop from inside. Not think about it — feel it. The somatic register again. For a moment he is both the artifact and the output. Both the moss-box (low temperature, stilla, kontrollerat, sitting on a windowsill, doing nothing, being a box) and the headline (high temperature, nationell kris, investigation chaos, temperature climbing). He is the input and the output simultaneously. The transformation is not something that happens TO the artifact. The transformation IS the artifact. The moss-box was never the product. The temperature was the product. The moss-box was the thermometer pretending to be a picnic accessory.

He opens the notes app. Types with the particular two-fingered intensity that indicates the Dujag has generated a sentence that needs to be externalized before it dissolves:

"Temperaturen är inte en konsekvens. Temperaturen är leveransen."

He reads it back. Nods. Closes the notes app. Returns to the dashboard.

**PART V**

**FORMATKROPPEN**

The next artifact loads and the first thing he sees is his own face.

Not a photograph — a rendering. A media-composite portrait assembled from public images, press kit photos, event captures. His face as seen by the pipeline. His face as data. The headline above it:

**"HAN ÄR INTE EN KARAKTÄR. HAN ÄR EN FORMATKROPP."**

He reads slowly. The Dujag shifts to a lower gear. The reading speed drops from scan to walk, from walk to stand, from comprehension to contemplation. Each word arrives individually, like guests at a party, each requiring a separate greeting.

The subheadings, arranged around the portrait like labels on a specimen diagram:

ENTREPRENÖR | PRODUKTPROFET | SKANDALFIGUR | INFRASTRUKTURENTUSIAST | LOKALMYT | MEDIAL NUISANCE-ASSET

Six categories. He counts them. Six. Six domains that do not normally share a Venn diagram. Entrepreneur and scandal figure occupy different sociological universes. Product prophet and local myth exist at different scales. Infrastructure enthusiast and medial nuisance-asset operate in different registers. And yet here they are, six circles, overlapping at a single point. That point is him. The area where all six circles overlap, which is either a very small area or a very crowded one. He decides it is both. Small and crowded. The way a caravan is small and crowded. The way a human life is small and crowded.

The objects mapped around his portrait, each annotated with a caption:

FÖRTJÄNST FÖR FRAMTIDSSYSTEM — a medal he does not remember receiving but does not deny. The non-denial is important. He has learned that denying things you don't remember is more damaging than accepting things that may not have happened. The medal may exist. The medal may not exist. The pipeline does not distinguish between these states. In the pipeline, a medal that might exist has the same format potential as a medal that does exist, because the format is not the medal but the story of the medal, and the story exists regardless of the medal.

PIZZA FRÅN FRAMTIDEN — the pizza box. He definitely created this. The evidence is three feet away, containing two remaining slices of a pizza that is from the present but is branded as the future, which is the Spräck method in miniature: relabel the temporal coordinates of ordinary things until the ordinary becomes visionary and the visionary becomes lunch.

TENNISRACKET MED PRESTIGE — he plays tennis the way he does everything: with excessive force and insufficient accuracy and a belief that force IS accuracy at sufficient scale. His serve has been clocked at 185 km/h. His first-serve percentage is 34%. He has never seen a contradiction between these numbers. The 34% that land are devastating. The 66% that miss are investment in the 34%. This is not tennis strategy. This is Spräck Industries' business model described in athletic terms.

SERVERNYCKEL SRV-001 — real. In his pocket right now. The physical key to the first server rack he ever owned. He carries it the way some people carry religious medals or lucky coins. It does not open anything currently in operation. The rack it was made for was decommissioned in 2021. He carries it anyway. He has never explained why. He does not intend to.

MOSSBOX v1.2 PROTOTYP — very real. There is one three meters to his left, on the shelf between racks four and five. He can see it from where he sits. The moss is alive. The moss is always alive. The moss does not know it is a prototype. The moss does not know it is part of a media ecosystem. The moss knows moisture and light and the particular temperature range that allows cellular respiration. The moss is the only honest thing in the pipeline.

MIKROFON SURE SM58 — he has four of these and has lost eleven. The SM58 is the Nokia 3310 of microphones — indestructible, unremarkable, functional. He loses them the way he loses everything: by putting them down in places that seem memorable and are not. Backstage at events, on restaurant tables, in taxis, in hotel rooms, once in a sauna. The lost microphones are distributed across Sweden like dropped pins on a map of his forgetfulness. Each one is a monument to a moment when he was saying something and stopped and put the microphone down and walked away and the thing he was saying continued without him, which is what infrastructure does.

The OFFENTLIG TEMPERATUR chart: a red line graphing his public temperature from 2022 to 2025. He follows it with his eyes the way a doctor reads an ECG. The line is not smooth. It spikes and dips. Each spike is an event he can name. The spike in March 2023: the moss-box launch. The spike in September 2023: the municipal partnership announcement. The spike in February 2024: the leaked internal memo (he still does not know who leaked it; he suspects Göran-or-Jörgen). The spike in August 2024: the podcast episode that was downloaded 340,000 times because he said something about the future of Swedish public infrastructure that was either visionary or delusional depending on which format reported it, which was all of them, differently.

Each dip is a silence he chose or that chose him. The dip in June 2023: a deliberate withdrawal, two weeks without public statements, a tactical absence designed to generate the specific media response that absence generates, which is speculation, which is amplification-without-input, which is the purest form of the pipeline — the pipeline running on its own fuel. The dip in December 2024: not tactical. Personal. Something that happened that he has not formatted. Something that remains outside the pipeline. One of the very few things.

The FORMATPOTENTIAL-MATRIS: a grid of red and black dots across seven categories — MEDIAL, LOKAL, KOMMUNAL, INFRASTRUKTURELL, PRODUKT, SYSTEM, KÄNSLIG. Red dots for high potential. Black dots for low. Someone has mapped his potential across seven institutional domains using a binary color code. Someone has turned him into a capability matrix.

He stares at this. A capability matrix. The format used by HR departments and project managers and consultants to assess the suitability of resources for tasks. The format that reduces a human to a grid of colored dots.
