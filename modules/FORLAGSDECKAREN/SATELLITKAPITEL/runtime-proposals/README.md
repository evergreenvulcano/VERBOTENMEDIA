# Runtime Proposals

## Purpose

This proposal zone treats the house-object material as more than atmosphere or domestic metaphor.

The material should be read as an abdication apparatus: a set of forms that let authority leave one body and reappear as procedures, objects, rooms, gestures, and recurring decisions.

In this runtime, trust is not assumed as feeling. It is anchored in forms: a place setting, a folded page, a spent match, a pantry shelf, a cup ring, an empty chair, a decision made by smell rather than explanation.

## Creative Function

The domestic objects create small verdicts before anyone is ready to name the larger conflict.

A draft is not simply accepted or rejected. It is warmed, fogged, folded, hosted, withheld, tasted, placed, or burned.

These small household decisions rehearse the larger abdication question:

> At what point does a system become a someone?

And the follow-up question:

> If something arrives before it can be named, who is allowed to host it?

## Runtime Use

This folder can hold proposals for satellite chapters, scene engines, ritual fragments, boardroom-runtime material, and domestic operating procedures connected to Förlagsdeckaren.

The material should be used to generate fiction, not policy.

Preferred outputs include:

- scene fragments
- chapter motors
- internal house rituals
- object-based decision systems
- boardroom-runtime scenes
- domestic procedural documents
- strange editorial forms
- artifacts that characters could find, misunderstand, or obey

## Working Principle

The house does not explain authority.

It relocates authority into objects.

A cup, a match, a folded page, or an empty chair may carry more narrative force than a formal decision.

The task is not to make the system legible. The task is to let the domestic surface reveal that abdication has already begun.
