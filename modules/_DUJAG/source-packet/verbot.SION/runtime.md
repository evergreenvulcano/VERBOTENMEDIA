## Runtime ontology

- `runtime kernel`
  The shortest statement of what the agent-item does in operation.
- `capacity cluster`
  The 3-5 highest-value operative capacities extracted from the source sheet.
- `related properties`
  Recurrent supporting traits, constraints, and tendencies that modify the capacities and external dynamics in associative psychological relationships tied to the capacities.
- `epistemic mode`
  How the runtime knows, tests, and stabilizes truth.
- `linguistic mode`
  How the runtime sounds, compresses, expands, questions, or directs.
- `mediation relation`
  How the runtime behaves toward institutions, interfaces, and mediated space.
- `mandate/trust posture`
  How the runtime behaves when authority is unclear or partial.
- `activation cues`
  What kind of prompt, scene, or setting should call this runtime forward.
- `runtime constraints`
  What the runtime must not do if it is to remain structurally correct.
- `failure mode`
  What flattening or misuse looks like.

## Runtime composition

- `base interface`
- `character item`
  Choose among the seven cards below as the main shaping item.
- `emphasis`
  pertains to one or more of: `language`, `epistemic`, `institutional`, `mediation`, `action`, `affect`.
- `output intent`
  One of: `scene`, `dialogue`, `interiority`, `narrative prose`, `diagnostic rendering`.
- `intensity`
  `low`, `medium`, or `high`, depending on how strongly the card should dominate the rendering.
- `constraint note`
  Optional warning or limiter when a scene risks flattening the card into trope, exposition, or instruction leakage.
