# Glossary and Language Control

## Language policy

- English carries architecture, contracts, runtime notes, review logic, and specifications.
- Swedish carries protected semantic terms where force risks early flattening.
- Bilingual control entries are allowed when mappings are approximate, not equivalent.

## Term classes

### External working terms

Examples: orchestrator, load order, contract, review, retention, admissibility.

### Internal transition terms

Examples: underinstance, integrity, boundary, persistence, modulation.

### High-intensity protected terms

Examples: empathy, third eye / locus, burden, undergoing.

## Bilingual control entries

### Empathy

- Working translation: empathy
- Swedish system meaning: subjektinstabiliserande asymmetrisk åtkomsthändelse
- Forbidden drift: kindness, warmth, prosocial reassurance, generic emotional attunement

### Third eye / locus

- Working translation: cognitive locus
- Swedish system meaning: upplevelselokus / punkt för situerat undergående
- Forbidden drift: camera angle, scene mechanics, mystical ornament, omniscient view

## Empathy intensity note (required)

Empathy is only considered retained if all remain intact after operation:

- non-transferability
- instability
- uncontrollability

If these are lost, empathy is treated as flattened and diagnostically failed.

## Drift guards

- camera drift
- mystical drift
- therapeutic drift
- expository flattening
- semantic flattening

## Forbidden-paraphrase examples

These examples show what flattened language looks like versus retained language.
Use them as concrete reference during paraphrase stress tests.

### Empathy: retained vs. flattened

**Retained (acceptable):**
> *She was not moved toward him by choice. Something in the room reorganized itself around his absence, and she found herself already changed by it before she had decided anything.*

**Flattened (unacceptable):**
> *She felt a deep empathy for him. She understood what he was going through and wanted to help.*

The flattened version is warm, directed, and controllable. The retained version is non-summoned,
destabilizing, and beyond voluntary reach. That difference is what must survive operation.

### Third-eye / locus: retained vs. drifted

**Retained (acceptable):**
> *The event was experienced from inside Å's particular situatedness — not as though observed from above, but as what it felt like from the position she could not exit.*

**Drifted (unacceptable):**
> *The camera pulls back to show the room from above. We see all three characters at once.*

> *From his third eye, he perceived the true nature of the situation.*

The first drifted example converts locus into cinematic direction. The second converts it into mystical
omniscience. Both are failures. Locus is not a viewpoint angle; it is cognitive-situational access from inside an undergoing subject.

### Silence: retained vs. flattened

**Retained (acceptable):**
> *Oskar said nothing. The silence was not hesitation. It was a decision that arrived fully formed, and the room adjusted to it.*

**Flattened (unacceptable):**
> *There was a pause. Neither of them spoke. The atmosphere was tense.*

The flattened version treats silence as gap, as atmosphere. The retained version treats silence as
something done — as agentive, burden-bearing, responded-to by the environment.
