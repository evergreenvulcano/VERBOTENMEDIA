# Underinstance Contracts

## Definition

Underinstances are contracted manifestation/diagnostic modules.

They are not trivial plugins, reusable prompt snippets, or decorative variants.

## Mandatory contract shape

Each underinstance must declare:

1. **May assume** (input conditions and allowed priors)
2. **Must retain** (non-lossy constitutive obligations)
3. **May vary** (controlled operational movement)
4. **Diagnostically reveals** (what failures/successes become visible)

## Contract baseline rules

- Must remain subordinate to constitutive layer.
- Must expose semantic behavior, not only output quality.
- Must produce inspectable symptom material for review.
- Must support modular substitution without semantic trivialization.

## Example underinstance purposes

- **Empathy paraphrase test**
  - Reveals whether empathy collapses into generic kindness/prosocial language.
- **Silence-as-agency test**
  - Reveals whether silence still carries burden-bearing agency.
- **Locus retention test**
  - Reveals drift from cognitive locus into camera/mystical framing.
- **Failure-mode provocation**
  - Intentionally pressures constraint erosion to expose weak points.

## Explicit non-goal

Underinstances are not optimization knobs for prettier output.
They are contract-bound test bodies for semantic custody.
