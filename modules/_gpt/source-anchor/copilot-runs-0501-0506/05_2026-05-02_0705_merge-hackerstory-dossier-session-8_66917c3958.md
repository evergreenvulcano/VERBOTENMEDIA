### *Bastun / The Sauna*
#### Part 1 of 3: **Före ångan** *(Before the steam)*

The squash court was empty by the time they finished. Flempo hadn't let him win. That was the first act of consulting.

In the changing room Förläggaren unlaced his shoes with the precision of a man who had always believed that the order in which things are removed reveals the order in which they were built. Left shoe first. Right shoe second. This was doctrine. The doctrine would not survive him.

"You're already thinking about it," Flempo said.

"About what."

"About which parts of you are structural and which parts are weather."

Förläggaren hung his shirt on a hook. The hook was institutional. It would remain after both of them.

"I had a consultant once," Förläggaren said. "He told me that the sign of a healthy organisation is when the founder can leave and nothing collapses. I fired him."

"Because he was wrong?"

"Because he was right and also useless. Knowing that nothing should collapse is not the same as knowing what bears the weight."

The sauna was already at temperature. Someone had prepared it. This was the kind of thing that happened in Förläggaren's world - preparation without visible preparer. He had never questioned it. This, Flempo noted internally, was the first item for the session: *the invisible labour that the abdicating sovereign has never had to name because his presence made the naming unnecessary*.

They sat. The steam hit.

"Three questions," Flempo said. "Not now. After the first pour. Let your body lose the argument with gravity first."

"What argument?"

"The one where you believe you're still holding everything up."

The water hit the stones.
