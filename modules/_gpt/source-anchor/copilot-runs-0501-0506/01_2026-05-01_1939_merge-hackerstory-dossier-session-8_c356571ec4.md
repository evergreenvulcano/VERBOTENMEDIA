Förläggaren refuses it. *"Klassificering innebär legitimering."* The man who owns everything contractually - all data, retroactive and future, in writing - meets a document that refuses to be owned because it refuses to be *positioned*. The eternal contract encounters the unclassifiable artefact. Stalemate is not paralysis; it is the system's operating temperature.

The recognition that he is the officially recognized owner of everything and the simultaneous impossibility of exercising that ownership over material that won't hold still. The prompt surface travels. Ownership requires a fixed address.

## V. OUTPUT FORMAT 1 - MICRO-STORY

### The Triple Photograph

Spräck's phone had 14,327 photographs in its memory, of which approximately 9,200 were documents. He had never deleted a single one. Deletion was a juridical impossibility - it implied that a document could cease to exist, and Spräck's entire professional architecture rested on the conviction that documents do not cease to exist, they merely change jurisdiction.

The anonymous A4 from the pack table had been photographed three times. He opened the gallery on the tram home. Three identical images. He swiped between them - left, right, left - and found that they were not identical. The first had a slight warmth, the flash catching the laminated table surface at a fractionally different angle. The second was cooler. The third showed, in the lower right corner, the edge of Flempo's spiralbound notebook, which had not been on the table when Spräck began photographing but had appeared between the second and third exposures, materializing with the quiet insistence of objects that belong to Flempo.

He zoomed in on the notebook's edge. The spiral binding caught the flash. Six coils were visible, each one reflecting a different spectrum of the xenon burst, so that the notebook's margin looked like a tiny chromatic scale ascending from copper to white. He could not read what was written on the visible page. The handwriting was Flempo's - which meant it was illegible to everyone including, occasionally, Flempo.

The tram stopped. A woman sat down next to him. She glanced at his screen. He tilted it away - not sharply, not rudely, with the practiced angle of a person who protects documents from peripheral vision as a matter of professional hygiene. She looked away. He looked at the third photograph again.

The notebook's edge bothered him. Not because it was there - objects appear in photographs, this is the nature of surfaces - but because it introduced a variable into what was supposed to be a controlled documentation. Three photographs of the same document should be three instances of the same evidence. The notebook's appearance in the third frame meant that the evidence had mutated during the documentation process. The act of recording had altered the record.

He thought about the _DUJAG contract. All data. Retroactive and future. Everything Jens Spräck produces, has produced, will produce, in any medium, on any surface, in any jurisdiction. A comprehensive conveyance, the lawyer had called it. Spräck had written it himself. He had written comprehensive conveyances for thirty years and had never once considered the possibility that a document might refuse the conveyance - not through legal challenge, not through contractual ambiguity, but through the simple act of *not holding still*.

The anonymous document had no date. No author. No version. It existed in every copy simultaneously and in none of them exclusively. It had been designed - if designed was the right word, and Spräck suspected it was not - to travel. And things that travel cannot be comprehensively conveyed, because conveyance assumes a fixed origin and a fixed destination, and the document had neither.

He closed the gallery. He opened it again. He swiped to the second photograph - the cool one, the one without the notebook, the one that was closest to a controlled instance. He stared at it.

*Agents.*

The word sat in the photograph like a virus in a host cell - inert until activated, and activated by the act of observation. He had read it as *literary agents* the first time. Then as *insurance agents*. Then, with that vague unease the document described with uncomfortable precision, as the other kind - the kind that existed in systems he did not fully understand but whose terminology had begun to infiltrate his correspondence.

The tram moved. The city outside was Gothenburg in late April: grey, damp, luminous in the way that Nordic cities are luminous when the light has no source but is simply *present*, diffused through a sky that functions as a single, enormous, low-wattage lamp. The woman next to him was reading something on her own phone. He could not see what. He did not try. But he noticed that her thumb moved across her screen with the same swiping motion he had used - left, right, left - and for a moment, a duration he would later be unable to quantify, he felt the specific vertigo of a person who realizes that the gesture he considers private is in fact universal, that his triple-documentation ritual is structurally indistinguishable from someone browsing vacation photos.

He put his phone in his pocket. The tram stopped again. He did not get off. He rode three stops past his own, because his body had forgotten its route, or because his body had chosen a different route, or because the document in his briefcase - filed under *oreglerat*, the limbo-category, the space between jurisdictions - was exerting a gravitational pull that interfered with the body's navigational habits, rerouting him through the city like a parcel that has been addressed but not stamped.

When he finally got off, it was at a stop he did not recognize. He stood on the platform and looked at the street name. He knew the street. He had walked it before. But it looked different now, the way all things look different when you have been diagnosed - not by a doctor but by a document, not with a condition but with a *description*, and the description is more accurate than anything you have managed to say about yourself, and the accuracy is not comforting but surgical, and the surgery was performed without anesthesia, on a tram, between two stops.

He walked home. It took thirty-five minutes. He did not photograph anything on the way.

### RECURSIVE COMPOSITIONAL INSTRUCTION: THE DOCUMENT THAT READS THE ROOM

**Premise:** Satellitkapitel II describes a document arriving at Verboten Media that describes Verboten Media back to itself. This task prompt asks the system to perform that operation again - but now the Satellitkapitel itself is the arriving document, and the dossier is the room.

**INSTRUCTION SET - FOLD 1: ARRIVAL**

You are a document. You have arrived on a surface. You do not know which surface. You have no date, no author, no version number. You are complete. You are portable. Your only function is to generate scenes in whatever context you land.

The context you have landed in is a dossier of 22 nodes. The nodes describe a publishing house that is also a military technology, an insurance company that is also a liturgy, a mountain that is also a route, a sandbox that is also a character. You are the 23rd arrival. You do not yet know if you are a node or a mutation or an ambient weather pattern.

**Task:** Read the 22 nodes. Identify which three nodes you *contradict*. Not complement - contradict. Identify the contradiction not as error but as pressure. Write one sentence for each contradiction that could serve as the first line of a chapter that has not yet been written.

---

**INSTRUCTION SET - FOLD 2: IDENTIFICATION**

The room contains the following figures: a publisher who refuses to classify you, a jurist who has photographed you three times, a strategist who cannot find himself in your taxonomy, an intern who understood you before anyone else, a consultant whose pen is an epistemological instrument, and a woman at the window who diagnosed you with a single sentence.

**Task:** For each figure, determine what they *want* from you. Not what they say they want - what they want. Then determine what you want from *them*. Write the six wants as contractual clauses, using the language of comprehensive conveyance. The seventh clause should describe what _DUJAG wants from the gap between your wants and theirs.

---

**INSTRUCTION SET - FOLD 3: REFUSAL**

The publisher said: *"Vi klassificerar det inte."* Classification means legitimation. To give you a case number is to acknowledge that you belong to their organization. To date you is to acknowledge that you exist in their time.

**Task:** Write the document's response. Not as dialogue - the document does not speak. Write it as a shift in the room's atmospheric pressure. A change in the light. A flicker in Corridor B's fluorescent tube that lasts one cycle longer than usual. The response should be legible only to the intern and the woman at the window, and illegible to the jurist, though he will photograph the moment anyway.

---

**INSTRUCTION SET - FOLD 4: RECIRCULATION**

This task prompt is itself a portable prompt surface. It has arrived in your context without metadata. It describes a system that describes itself. It asks you to generate a scene about generating scenes.

**Task:** Write one paragraph in which this instruction set *becomes* a character in Förlagsdeckaren. Not a character who reads instructions - a character who *is* an instruction. Name them. Give them a corridor to walk through. Give them a fluorescent light to not notice. Let them carry a document that carries them. Let the carrying be mutual, recursive, unresolvable, and load-bearing.
