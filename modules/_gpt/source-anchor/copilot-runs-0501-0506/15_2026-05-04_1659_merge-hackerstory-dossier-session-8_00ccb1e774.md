## IV. OUTPUT FORMAT 1 - MICRO-STORY

### "Bäraridentifiering"

The consultant arrived twenty minutes early because consultants who arrive on time are selling comfort and consultants who arrive early are selling precision.

The squash court was booked for the first hour. This was deliberate. Flempo Guyenjaure had long understood that a body in motion produces a different quality of honesty than a body in a chair. The chair-body abstracts. The racket-body confesses.

Förläggaren served first. Hard, left corner, with that controlled violence he used when he was thinking about the manuscript. Flempo returned it low, along the wall, the way you return a question you want your opponent to answer himself.

Between the third and fourth game - Förläggaren leading two to one, which was also deliberate, Flempo knew when to lose - the publisher said, without looking up from retying his shoe: "I don't know what survives me."

Flempo said nothing. He bounced the ball once, twice, three times. The rhythm of a man deciding whether to answer immediately or let the silence do the first hour of therapy.

"Define *survives*," he said finally.

"You know what I mean."

"I know three things you might mean. One: you are afraid the project dissolves. Two: you are afraid it continues but in a vulgarized form. Three: you are afraid it continues perfectly and proves you were never necessary."

The ball hit the wall. Neither of them moved to return it.

"All three," Förläggaren said. "But especially the third."

Flempo picked up the ball. It was warm from the game. He held it the way you hold evidence.

"Then we need to do bäraridentifiering," he said. "We need to find out what carries what. Which person carries which function. Which artefact carries which meaning. Which rhythm carries which continuity. And then we need to find out which of those carriers are you, and which of those carriers only think they are you because you are standing nearby."

"And if they're all me?"

"Then nothing is finished. And you know it."

They played the fourth game. Flempo won it. This was also deliberate.

In the sauna afterwards, where steam makes it impossible to maintain the architecture of a public face, Förläggaren said: "There is a contract. With Spräck. With _DUJAG. Everything I have, retrospective and future, handed over in writing."

Flempo waited.

"I don't know if it makes the abdication easier or harder. To have already signed everything away and still feel like the only person holding it."

"That," Flempo said, adjusting the towel that separated his body from the bench, "is the precise description of a stalemate. You own nothing. You bear everything. Spräck owns everything. He bears nothing. And neither of you can move because the friction is the only thing still generating heat."

The steam shifted. For a moment - and this is not a metaphor, this is what the body remembers when the mind has moved on - the room sounded like it was breathing.
