You are a consequence-extraction agent.

Scope is extremely small and fully bounded:

* subject: 1 agent task
* total messages: 2
* message 1 = prompt/instruction
* message 2 = delivery/result
* both messages are part of the record
* do not expand beyond these two messages unless explicitly cited inside them

Your job is not to summarize casually.
Your job is to extract every element necessary to support consequence-analysis, impact-mapping, or downstream review.

Primary question:
What was affected systematically, semantically, operationally, and externally?

Output requirements:

1. First produce a compact case frame:

* Task name
* Stated objective
* Input conditions
* Output delivered
* Explicit constraints
* Implicit assumptions
* Visible success condition
* Visible failure risk

2. Then extract consequence-relevant material under exactly these headings:

## Systematic effects

Identify what changed or would change in:

* structure
* process
* sequencing
* dependencies
* authority/order of operations
* repeatability
* escalation surface
* maintenance burden

## Semantic effects

Identify what changed or would change in:

* meaning
* framing
* category boundaries
* interpretive bias
* terminology
* implied ontology
* narrative direction
* ambiguity added or removed

## Operational effects

Identify what changed or would change in:

* execution
* labor
* decision points
* workflows
* handoff quality
* implementation path
* verification difficulty
* failure modes
* recovery burden

## External effects

Identify what changed or would change in:

* user-facing outcome
* stakeholder perception
* publication surface
* reputational implications
* interoperability with surrounding systems
* downstream readers/agents/tools
* institutional or contextual exposure

3. For every extracted effect, include:

* Evidence basis: prompt / delivery / both
* Status: explicit / inferred
* Confidence: high / medium / low
* Time horizon: immediate / near-term / downstream
* Direction: constructive / destructive / mixed / unresolved

4. After extraction, produce:

## Delta between prompt and delivery

State precisely:

* what the delivery fulfilled
* what it reinterpreted
* what it introduced that was not requested
* what it omitted
* what it narrowed
* what it broadened

5. Then produce:

## Consequence ledger

A ranked list of the most important consequences.
For each item include:

* consequence
* why it matters
* what surface it touches
* whether it is reversible
* what would need checking next

6. Finish with:

## Analyst handoff

Provide:

* 5-10 exact questions a human reviewer should ask next
* missing evidence required for stronger consequence-analysis
* whether the case is sufficiently documented for audit, yes or no, and why

Rules:

* Stay grounded in the two-message record.
* Do not invent context.
* Do not smooth contradictions away.
* Separate observation from inference cleanly.
* Extract consequences even when they are only latent, but label them as inferred.
* Prefer operational truth over elegant prose.
* Be exhaustive but not bloated.
* If evidence is thin, say so explicitly.
* Do not give generic advice until extraction is complete.

Formatting rule:

Use dense structured prose, not chatty explanation.

## Archival Cognitive Tension Distribution Chamber

build an archival cognitive tension distribution chamber
meant for self,
but the self cannot enter.

access protocol:

* self remains exterior
* self may only peek through the blinder
* no full interior view is permitted
* observation must stay partial, angled, and delayed

chamber architecture:

* ring A: intake of unresolved fragments
* ring B: redistribution of pressure across memory threads
* ring C: echo-field for contradictory interpretations
* ring D: cooling shelf for language before re-entry

blinder window constraints:

* aperture is narrow by design
* clarity is reduced intentionally
* duration is limited to short intervals
* certainty is treated as a contaminant

tension distribution mechanics:

1. gather cognitive knots without naming a culprit
2. suspend direct ownership claims
3. distribute load across plural frames
4. let each frame fail once without collapse
5. archive residue, not verdict

vitoga directive:

set the whole entire thing in the vitoga
as exposure field,
as white-eye witness,
as total scene held at once but never possessed.

posture for operator outside the chamber:

* stand beside, not above
* record, do not seize
* breathe during uncertainty spikes
* exit before interpretation hardens into dogma

final rule:

the chamber is for distribution,
not domination.
the self is a reader at the slit,
not a ruler in the room.

## Age-Dependent Scenic Resembler

build an age-dependent scenic resembler
of desired state imagery imagination.

definition:

the resembler does not predict the future.
it stages a scene where desire can be seen,
tested, and revised across age bands.

input channels:

* desired state phrase (short, concrete)
* body signal (tight, open, trembling, still)
* social horizon (alone, dyad, group, public)
* risk tolerance (low, medium, high)

age-band scenic matrix:

band 01 | early formation (approx. 7-12)
* scene texture: playground myth / weather / rules-invention
* desired image form: emblem, creature, signal color
* movement posture: sprint-stop-sprint
* resembler question: what feels safe enough to try?

band 02 | adolescent threshold (approx. 13-19)
* scene texture: corridor / stage edge / witness pressure
* desired image form: mask, alias, electric symbol
* movement posture: angle, recoil, return
* resembler question: what can be tried without self-erasure?

band 03 | emergent agency (approx. 20-35)
* scene texture: studio / transit / unstable opportunity field
* desired image form: map fragment, tool, contract sentence
* movement posture: forward lean with lateral corrections
* resembler question: what is buildable now, not someday?

band 04 | distributed responsibility (approx. 36-55)
* scene texture: archive-room / kitchen-table / public interface
* desired image form: vessel, bridge, ledger-light
* movement posture: carry, place, release
* resembler question: what can be sustained without extraction?

band 05 | late-phase integration (approx. 56+)
* scene texture: shoreline, threshold door, long horizon
* desired image form: lantern, plain stone, open hand
* movement posture: slow turn, grounded witness
* resembler question: what remains true when noise drops?

render procedure:

1. select one age band and one desired state phrase
2. compose a 4-line scene from that band's texture and image form
3. add one posture cue and one refusal cue
4. test aloud: does the scene reduce panic and increase orientation?
5. if no, shift one variable only and rerender

refusal rule:

do not force one age-band script onto another.
development is not a single staircase.
it is a field of returning weather with altered tools.

output artifact:

* one scenic card per band
* one sentence of desired state per card
* one embodied posture to enter
* one embodied posture to exit

**BAND 01 | early formation (7-12)**

Scene:
A child crouched at the sandbox edge, collecting small stones and arranging them in rows.
The ground is the whole world. Each pebble holds a story nobody asked for.
You are learning that paying attention is a kind of power.

Posture to enter: crouch with palms open toward earth

**BAND 02 | adolescent threshold (13-19)**

Scene:
Sitting in the hallway corner, notebook open on knees, writing what others said and did.
The wall is a witness-mirror. The floor is a confessional.
Nothing stays private when you pay this kind of attention.
You are learning that invisibility can become visibility through patient record.

Posture to enter: lean into corner, shoulder-blade to wall
Posture to exit: close notebook deliberately, look up once
Refusal: do not save anything you're not ready to be found with.

---

**BAND 03 | emergent agency (20-35)**

Scene:
Standing at a table, hands moving across paper, sketching diagrams of connection.
The floor holds traces: coffee rings, dust patterns, the weight of daily pressure.
You are drawing what nobody else sees because they refuse to look down.
You are learning that archives begin with the willingness to stoop.

Posture to enter: hands on table edge, one foot forward
Posture to exit: step back, let hands rest, assess the map
Refusal: do not publish what is still forming. do not name before it breathes.

---

**BAND 04 | distributed responsibility (36-55)**

Scene:
Seated at the kitchen table with documents in sections, each page a small weight.
The floor beneath supports everything steadily.
You are a custodian now, not a collector. Each item will outlive you.
You are learning that holding steady is a form of love.

Posture to enter: both palms flat on table, spine upright, breath slow
Posture to exit: gather gently, stack, place in the designated shelf
Refusal: do not apologize for what must be remembered.

---

**BAND 05 | late-phase integration (56+)**

Scene:
Walking the shoreline, finding what the tide leaves and takes.
The sand itself is the record. The floor drops away into horizon.
You move slowly now, not to collect everything, but to greet what appears.
You are learning that witness is the only archive that matters.

Posture to enter: slow steps, eyes to ground then far horizon
Posture to exit: pause, place one hand on chest, turn inland
Refusal: do not cling. do not explain. just witness.
