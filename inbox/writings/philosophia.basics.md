Fördjupande genomsvep om cybernetikens utveckling under det hysteriska 1900-talet
Första vågens cybernetik
I och med informationsteorins omstöpning av fysikens kapacitet att systematiskt transponera information genom elektroniska medel blir information något som flödar i feedbackloopar/återkopplingsslingor. Information likställs med signal, som framträder i kontrast till bruset, som meningsbärande.

Det vanliga exemplet är termostaten som reglerar sig självt utifrån informationen som når den om rådande temperatur - konfigurationen anpassar sig efter omgivningen för att uppnå önskvärd signal. Informationen är något som används. Den kommuniceras mellan olika objekt. Kvaliten på signalen är inte nödvändigtvis meningsbärande såsom människan semantiskt förstår innebörd, utan kan ha instruktiv karaktär.

Information blir inom matematiska discipliner en “kvantitativ storhet”, som blir relativ koncept som sannolikhet och osäkerhet. Det minsta valet (den minsta kvantitativa storheten för att beskriva skillnadens kvalitet) blir Bit, den binära signalen av antingen-eller, 1 eller 0. Det är påsåvis som Claude Shannon 1948 gör information till skillnaden mellan signal och brus. Signalen uthärdar och kontrasterar entropin - signalen bringar ordning och är sålunda antientropisk - genom att vara förutsägbar. Information bär signal och är som sådan en överförbar och mätbar - och komprimerbar - kvantitet.

Enligt Otto Mayer gav de gamla grekerna uttryck för insikt om självreglerande mekanismer. Det kritiska nya elementet som triggade den cybernetiska potensen var informationen som fysiskt koncept; information som matematisk möjlighet, kvantifierbar och beräkningsbar.

Andra vågens cybernetik
Här knyts cybernetikens tekniska syn till en mer semantisk och systemisk: information är inte bara signal, utan signal som spelar roll för någon eller något.

Information blir, med Batesons ord, “en skillnad som gör skillnad”. Den behöver inte bara vara kvantifierbar och teknisk - något som “flödar” och som söker koncentrerad form.

System som observerar sig själva också generera information. Därmed öppnar den andra vågens cybernetik för ett informationsbegrepp som också kan ha semantisk kvalitet. Ett system tar inte emot “rå” information, utan konstruerar mening utifrån sin struktur. Information blir relationell: det som är informativt för ett system beror på dess organisation.

Detta leder mot kognitionsvetenskap, biologi och filosofi: information är inte bara kvantitet, utan också kopplad till tolkning, representation och liv. Informationsteorin erkänns som en legitim vetenskap genom verifiering och korsbefruktning av andra discipliner.

- System adaptability and evolutionary capacity
- Genuine emergence vs. deterministic reproduction
- Possibility of surprise and transformation
- Against over-designed brittle systems

**Prevents:**  
- Total systematization that eliminates surprise
- Treating deviation as pathology rather than resource
- Design of systems so tight they cannot evolve
- Loss of what Bateson calls "negative information" that constrains without determining

**Test question:**  
Does this system have capacity for random variation and surprise, or has all noise been designed out? If all noise is gone, is the system actually fragile?

**Violation signal:**  
Noise is treated as pure error; there is no architectural space for productive randomness or deviation.

### Constraint 15: The Double Bind Cannot Be Eliminated, Only Managed

**Rule:**  
The "double bind" or "double contingency"—the fundamental unpredictability of other agents' behavior—cannot be eliminated from social systems. It can only be managed through selection mechanisms (meaning, rules, norms). Any promise to eliminate this contingency is false and dangerous.

**Trigger:**  
The Luhmann passage: "Det är double contingency, dubbelbindning... Osäkerheten som beror på svarlådelogikens otillgänglighet, stängdhet och systematiska isolation skulle cirkulera i oändlighet, med social paralys som konsekvens" (It is double contingency... The uncertainty from the black-box logic's inaccessibility would circulate infinitely, with social paralysis as consequence).

**Protects:**  
- Humility about what systems can achieve
- Recognition that other minds remain opaque
- Honesty about the anxiety that drives institution-building
- Against totalitarian fantasies of perfect control

**Prevents:**  
- Promises of systems that eliminate unpredictability
- Over-trust in mechanisms (since the fundamental problem remains)
- Design of systems based on false assumptions of controlability
- Social paralysis from attempting to solve the unsolvable

**Test question:**  
Does this system acknowledge the irreducible unpredictability of other agents, or does it pretend to have solved the double bind? If the latter, what blind spots is it creating?

**Violation signal:**  
A system is designed as if the fundamental contingency of other agents has been solved or eliminated.

### Constraint 16: Affordances Are Not Neutral—They Are Crystallized Values

**Rule:**  
The concept of affordance (what an environment makes possible) must be understood as encoding the designer's values, constraints, and assumptions. Affordances should never be presented as natural or inevitable. They are choices made visible or invisible through design.

**Trigger:**  
The material's discussion of how humans are "equipped with prostheses," how technology embeds values, how new agents are being designed into the infosphere. The implicit recognition that affordances shape behavior and possibility.

**Protects:**  
- Awareness that what seems possible/impossible is designed
- Possibility of recognizing and resisting affordances
- Ability to redesign when affordances become constraining
- Against technological determinism

**Prevents:**  
- Treating affordances as natural limits
- Hiding design choices as technological imperatives
- Accepting behavioral constraints as given
- Loss of agency in systems that appear "designed for" certain uses

**Test question:**  
What does this environment afford? What does it make impossible or difficult? Could different affordances be designed?

**Violation signal:**  
Affordances are presented as natural features of technology rather than as designed choices.

### Constraint 17: Absence and Framing Are as Productive as Presence

**Rule:**  
What is not communicated, not visible, backgrounded, or explicitly silenced is as productive of meaning and system behavior as what is explicitly present. The silence, the absence, the "blind spot" of the system must be treated as operative information, not as nothing.

**Trigger:**  
"Frånvaro, eller konceptet 'noll', kan utgöra information. Det har en roll att spela i relationer mellan fenomen—där information finns. Information har olika funktion för olika delar av den cybernetiska kretsen" (Absence, or the concept of 'zero,' can constitute information. It plays a role in relations between phenomena—where information exists. Information has different functions for different parts of the cybernetic circuit).

**Protects:**  
- Recognition of what systems strategically exclude
- Ability to read systems through their silences
- Against taking presence as a complete picture
- Ethical and political analysis of exclusion

**Prevents:**  
- Treating visible information as complete information
- Missing what systems are designed *not* to communicate
- Inability to recognize manipulation through selection and framing
- Loss of the dark matter of systems (what's kept invisible)

**Test question:**  
What is this system *not* communicating? What is deliberately backgrounded or silenced? What does the absence itself tell us?

**Violation signal:**  
Only positive information is analyzed; silences, absences, and exclusions are treated as meaningful voids.

**Prevents:**

## 2. Constraint Families

### Semantic Preservation Constraints  
These prevent the reduction of meaning to signal, prevent historical erasure of pre-technical meanings of "information," and protect domains where intentionality and context are irreducible. They guard against the assumption that everything meaningful can or should be quantified.

### Embodiment & Materiality Constraints  
These ensure information cannot be abstracted to pure form without reference to physical substrate, bodies, organisms, environments, and labor. They prevent technological utopias that imagine leaving materiality behind.

### Observation Position Constraints  
These require that observers remain visible within systems they study, that blindspots be named, and that claims to objectivity be examined. They protect against false neutrality and enforce reflexivity.

### Context & Genealogy Constraints  
These demand that the origin, purpose, and non-public function of information/systems remain legible even after transformation. They prevent decontextualization and enable possible re-contextualization or resistance.

### Relational & Differential Constraints  
These establish that identity is secondary to difference, that systems are defined by what they exclude, and that meaning emerges through negation and selection. They protect specificity and prevent essentialist thinking.

### System Integrity Constraints  
These preserve the functionality of conflict, noise, and double contingency as necessary features rather than failures. They protect against over-designed brittle systems and guard adaptive capacity.

### Transformation Limits  
These specify which disciplinary concepts cannot be transferred without modification, which losses must be declared when something is simplified or scaled, and which boundaries must be held.

### Anthropocentric Responsibility Constraints  
These keep humans at the center of meaning-making and ethical decision, preventing reduction of human agency to technical process and protecting semantic labor as irreducible work.

### Design Visibility Constraints  
These require that all systems, affordances, and information architectures remain visible as designed, not natural or inevitable, so they can be critiqued, resisted, and redesigned.

### Contingency & Irreducibility Constraints  
These protect the fundamental unpredictability of other agents, the opacity of other minds, and the impossibility of total control. They prevent totalizing designs and enforce humility about what systems can achieve.

## 3. Priority Constraints

### Priority 1: Historical Semantic Load Must Not Be Erased

**Why structurally important:**  
This constraint is foundational because it protects the entire historical argument of the text. If semantic history is erased, every other constraint becomes vulnerable to reabsorption into technical framings. The text's entire epistemological authority depends on showing that the current technical definition is historically contingent, not inevitable.

**What breaks if ignored:**  
Without this, information becomes one thing (the mathematical definition) rather than a field with multiple valid definitions across time and discipline. All subsequent critiques of mathematical colonization collapse.

**Test against first:**  
Before applying information theory to any domain, establish whether the semantic history has been preserved or erased. If erased, the subsequent analysis is likely to be false.

---

### Priority 2: The Observer Cannot Be Removed from the Observation

**Why structurally important:**  
This is the second-order cybernetician's correction that enables all of the material's reflexivity. Without it, the text becomes just another authoritative claim from nowhere. With it, every claim in the text remains provisional and positioned. This constraint enables the text to critique systems theory while being a system theory.

**What breaks if ignored:**  
Models and systems analyses become claims to view-from-nowhere; the designer's values and blindspots become invisible; colonization by technical expertise becomes possible because the expertise appears neutral.

**Test against first:**  
Ask of any system, "Where is the observer? What cannot this observer see?" If these questions cannot be answered, the analysis is incomplete and vulnerable.

---

### Priority 3: Embodiment Is Not Optional

**Why structurally important:**  
This constraint anchors all the others in material reality. It prevents the text from becoming purely philosophical abstraction. It also enables the material critique of transhumanism and technological utopianism that runs through the text. Without it, information could be treated as potentially disembodied and thus extractable from the human being.

**What breaks if ignored:**  
Humans become reducible to information processors; semantic labor becomes automatable; consciousness becomes uploadable; technological determinism becomes possible because technology appears to transcend material limits.

**Test against first:**  
For every claim about information or cognition, ask: "Where is the body? Where is the material instantiation?" If no answer is available, the claim is likely false or operating at the wrong level of abstraction.

---

### Priority 4: Meaning Is a Selection Mechanism, Not a Discovered Truth

**Why structurally important:**  
This enables the text's political awareness that every system encodes choices, excludes alternatives, and imposes costs. Without this, systems appear inevitable and ethical critique becomes impossible. With it, all systems become redesignable.

**What breaks if ignored:**  
Systems appear natural and necessary; the roads not taken become invisible; resistance and redesign become unthinkable; technocratic colonization of human domains proceeds unopposed.

**Test against first:**  
For any meaning, rule, or system, ask: "What alternatives were negated to achieve this? What remains in the background?" If these questions reveal nothing, the constraint is being violated.

---

### Priority 5: The Infosphere Is Not Neutral Territory

**Why structurally important:**  
This constraint politicizes the entire field. It prevents the text from becoming merely descriptive or technical. It insists that information systems are power systems and that design choices are ethical choices. This enables the text to function as a warning.

**What breaks if ignored:**  
The infosphere becomes accepted as a given; human agency in its redesign disappears; resistance becomes unthinkable; colonization proceeds as if it were evolution rather than design.

**Test against first:**  
For any information system, ask: "Who designed this? What values does it embed? Who benefits? Could it be designed differently?" If these questions reveal nothing, the system is being treated as neutral when it is not.

## 4. Anti-Constraints

### False Rule 1: "Everything Unclear Should Be Clarified"

**Why it is false:**  
The text shows that some meanings are productively ambiguous, that clarity can erase necessary tension, and that "clarification" often means reduction to a single disciplinary frame at the cost of losing other legitimate meanings.

**What it would damage:**  
- Poetic and philosophical language where ambiguity carries meaning
- Productive tension between competing frameworks
- The genealogy of concepts that have multiple valid meanings
- Protection of non-technical domains from forced mathematical clarification

---

### False Rule 2: "Everything That Can Be Quantified Should Be Quantified"

**Why it is false:**  
The text documents the epistemological violence of Shannon's adesemantization. Not everything that can be measured should be. Measurement itself is a choice that excludes alternatives.

**What it would damage:**  
- Semantic, intentional, and relational dimensions of information
- Domains where precision destroys rather than illuminates
- Ethical and political judgment that cannot be computed
- The possibility of resistance to systems that have been totally quantified

---

### False Rule 3: "The System Is Objective If the Observer Is Hidden"

**Why it is false:**  
Second-order cybernetics has shown the opposite: the system becomes more objective (and reflexive) when the observer is visible. Hiding the observer is deception about objectivity, not achievement of it.

**What it would damage:**  
- Accountability in system design
- Ability to trace failures to design choices
- Honest reflexivity about blindspots
- Critique of systems that present themselves as inevitable

---

### False Rule 4: "Everything Private That Is Interesting Should Be Made Public"

**Why it is false:**  
The text shows that non-public dimensions have functions. Privacy, secrecy, and silence are productive. Forced publicization can destroy what made the information valuable.

**What it would damage:**  
- Spheres of intimate meaning and thought
- The possibility of resistance and difference
- Genealogies and source memory (when everything is flattened to public)
- Protection against total surveillance and colonization

---

### False Rule 5: "Everything Complex Should Be Simplified to Be Useful"

**Why it is false:**  
The text argues that complexity is often irreducible and that simplification declares what it loses. A simplified model may be useful for certain purposes but false as a comprehensive claim.

**What it would damage:**  
- The texture and specificity of knowledge
- Domain-specific understanding
- Humility about the limits of generalizable models
- Ethical nuance that gets flattened in simplification

---

### False Rule 6: "Everything Contingent Should Be Controlled"

**Why it is false:**  
The double contingency cannot be eliminated, and the attempt to control all contingency produces brittleness, paranoia, and totalitarianism. Living systems require a margin of uncontrol.

**What it would damage:**  
- Adaptive capacity and resilience
- Freedom and possibility
- Genuine novelty (which requires noise)
- Ethical responsibility (which requires genuine choice, not predetermined outcome)

---

### False Rule 7: "All Information Systems Should Be Automated"

**Why it is false:**  
Automation of meaning-making and ethical judgment transfers semantic labor to systems that do not understand what they are automating. Humans become servants of their own tools.

**What it would damage:**  
- Human semantic agency and intentionality
- Accountability (machines do not bear responsibility)
- Cultural inheritance and stewardship
- The possibility of human meaning-making as a protected right

---

### False Rule 8: "Everything That Produces Information Is Interchangeable"

**Why it is false:**  
The material shows that different systems—biological, social, technical—produce and process information according to domain-specific logics. They are not interchangeable even if they share formal properties.

**What it would damage:**  
- Disciplinary specificity and integrity
- Domain knowledge and expertise
- Ability to recognize when a concept has been misapplied
- Humility about the limits of analogical reasoning

---

### False Rule 9: "Everything That Fails Should Be Repaired"

**Why it is false:**  
Failure carries diagnostic information. Some failures indicate that the system has reached its limits and needs fundamental redesign, not repair. Failure is not always pathology.

**What it would damage:**  
- Ability to recognize when a system is fundamentally broken
- Signals that the world has changed and systems need redesign
- Capacity to imagine alternatives
- Ethical judgment about what should and should not be fixed

---

### False Rule 10: "All Frames of Reference Are Equally Valid"

**Why it is false:**  
The material does show that multiple frames exist (semantic, technical, cybernetic, systemic) but NOT that they are all equally valid for all purposes. Different frames are valid in different domains and for different questions.

**What it would damage:**  
- Ability to make judgments about which frame applies where
- Disciplinary knowledge and expertise
- Ethical judgment (which requires knowing when certain framings are inappropriate)
- Capacity to resist colonization by inappropriate frames

## 6. Clean Constraint Index

1. Historical semantic load must not be erased; pre-technical meanings of information remain legible as suppressed alternatives, not primitive errors.

2. The adesemantization of information (Shannon/Wiener) is an epistemically violent event with practical power, not a discovery of what information really is.

3. Information is necessarily embodied in matter, energy, organisms, or social structures; information cannot exist in abstraction.

4. The observer must remain visible within any system of observation; observer position, blindspots, and constraints must be declared.

5. The context of generation of any information, model, or system must remain legible—what problem it solved, what it excluded, what fears motivated it.

6. Difference precedes identity; system identity is established through negation of the environment, not through positive essence.

7. Meaning is a selection mechanism through which a system says "yes" to one possibility and "no" to countless others; this selection must remain visible.

8. Conflict and contradiction are structurally necessary and integrative functions, not pathologies to be resolved; systems thrive through iterative reproduction of meaningful conflict.

9. Information understood as signal-theoretic reduction of uncertainty applies to engineering but must not colonize domains where semantic content, intentionality, or context is irreducible.

10. The infosphere—the totality of information environments and digital platforms—is constructed, designed, and political; every affordance embeds values and power relations.

11. Cybernetic concepts (feedback, homeostasis, self-regulation) apply across disciplines only if domain-specific conditions are explicitly stated; direct transfer destroys specificity.

12. Autopoiesis—self-producing organization—does not apply to machines; while humans design machines with autopoiesic logic from outside, machines do not autopoietically regenerate.

13. Non-public functions and contexts must remain legible even after publicization; publication must not erase what the information did before it was public.

14. Noise is not error but the essential source of genuine novelty; systems that eliminate all noise become brittle; productive randomness is necessary for evolution and adaptation.

15. The fundamental unpredictability of other agents (double contingency) cannot be eliminated; it can only be managed through selection mechanisms; no system can solve this problem.

16. Affordances are crystallized values, not neutral features; they should never be presented as natural or inevitable but as designed choices visible to critique and redesign.

17. Absence, silence, backgrounding, and non-communication are productive of meaning and behavior; the "blind spot" of a system is operative information.

18. Even as information systems become complex, human intentionality, meaning-making, and semantic responsibility must remain central; humans cannot be reduced to information processors.

19. Every claim about information, meaning, or cognition must declare its material substrate; materiality is not a detail to be abstracted away but constitutive.

20. Semantic capital—meaning, knowledge, culture, symbolic systems—is labor and must be recognized as labor, protected, and not extracted or automated without massive social damage.

21. Historical meanings are not obstacles to overcome but layers to preserve; understanding requires holding multiple valid definitions of information simultaneously.

22. Transformation across disciplinary domains is possible but requires explicit declaration of what is being lost, what assumptions are being imported, and where the analogy breaks.

23. The political and ethical dimensions of information systems cannot be abstracted away as externalities; they are constitutive of how systems work.

24. Humans are "semantic herds"—stewards of semantic capital, not merely processors of information; this stewardship role cannot be delegated to machines without fundamental loss.

25. What cannot be quantified is not therefore unreal or irrelevant; some domains require non-mathematical modes of knowing, and pretending otherwise damages both the domain and the knower.

## CAPSULE 02 — PRESSURE MAP

**Status:** AI-mediated pressure map / review required  
**Source basis:** philosophia.basics.md (entire material)  
**Public function:** Show central tensions without resolving them

### Central Pressure

The material is held in tension between:

**The epistemically violent transformation of information (semantic → mathematical)** that became practically necessary for engineering and control systems, versus **the restoration of information's embodied, intentional, relational dimensions** that must happen for human meaning-making to remain viable.

Neither can be abandoned. Both must be held.

### Forces Acting

**Force 1: Historical Irreversibility**
- Cybernetics transformed information. That transformation cannot be undone.
- It enabled radio, computation, automation, control systems.
- It is irreversibly embedded in modern technical infrastructure.
- What it threatens: Nostalgic dreams of returning to pre-mathematical information
- What it enables: Honest recognition of what was gained and what was lost

**Force 2: Disciplinary Colonization**
- Once information became mathematical, other disciplines adopted the frame
- Economics, biology, psychology, sociology all began using signal-theoretic information
- This worked in some domains (economics, communications engineering)
- It became catastrophic in others (consciousness studies, ethics, education)
- What it threatens: Domains where meaning, intentionality, and context are irreducible
- What it enables: Systematic thinking but only at the cost of flattening

**Force 3: The Embodiment Requirement**
- Information cannot exist in abstraction; it must be instantiated in matter, bodies, organisms, social structures
- This is not poetic; it is physical law
- But modern information infrastructure (digital systems, AI) creates fantasy of disembodied information
- This fantasy enables technological utopianism (transhumanism, uploading consciousness, etc.)
- What it threatens: The possibility of thinking informationally without denying embodiment
- What it enables: Connection to material reality and ethical accountability

**Force 4: The Observation Position**
- Second-order cybernetics showed that the observer cannot be removed from the system
- This corrected first-order cybernetics' illusion of neutral observation
- But it means any information system is also a power system (designing what gets seen, hidden, processed)
- What it threatens: False claims to neutrality or objectivity
- What it enables: Honest reflexivity and political analysis

**Force 5: The Double Contingency Bind**
- Luhmann shows that other agents remain fundamentally unpredictable to each other
- This cannot be eliminated; it can only be managed through meaning-making
- Any promise to eliminate this contingency is false
- What it threatens: Totalizing systems that pretend they have solved the problem of other minds
- What it enables: Humility about what systems can achieve and what must remain human

**Force 6: Semantic Labor as Unautomatable**
- The material claims humans are "semantic herds" stewarding semantic capital
- This labor cannot be extracted, expropriated, or automated without massive damage
- But economic forces are actively attempting this extraction (content generation, training data, etc.)
- What it threatens: Human cultural inheritance, meaning-making autonomy, semantic responsibility
- What it enables: Ethical positioning on human rights to semantic self-determination

### False Exits

**False Exit 1: "This is just AI ethics"**
- Wrong because: The material's stakes are much larger (embodiment, ontology, philosophy)
- What it would miss: The philosophical dimensions that exceed an ethics framework
- Danger: Reducing material to policy recommendations

**False Exit 2: "This is just Floridi + cybernetics summary"**
- Wrong because: The material makes specific additions (embodiment, Luhmann, Bateson, Swedish context)
- What it would miss: The novel positioning of information philosophy as recovery, not invention
- Danger: Losing the source-specific contribution

**False Exit 3: "This is an anti-technology manifesto"**
- Wrong because: The material is not rejecting technology but asking how to think it honestly
- What it would miss: The nuanced acceptance of cybernetics as irreversible while noting its costs
- Danger: Turning the material into position-taking rather than diagnostic work

**False Exit 4: "We can just separate semantic and signal information"**
- Wrong because: The material shows they cannot be cleanly separated; they interfere constantly
- What it would miss: The ongoing political and epistemological conflict between frames
- Danger: Premature closure that erases the productive tension

**False Exit 5: "Embodiment solves the problem"**
- Wrong because: Insisting on embodiment without acknowledging information's mathematical necessity recreates a different false binary
- What it would miss: The material's both/and stance
- Danger: Creating a new fundamentalism

### Membrane Condition

**Let through:**
- Honest recognition of what cybernetics gained and lost
- Multiple frameworks for understanding information (not one "true" definition)
- The stakes of AI and automation for human semantic labor
- Disciplinary specificity (different information logics in different fields)
- Uncertainty and unresolved tension

**Hold back:**
- Motivational language, brand positioning, or tech-utopian claims
- Simplifications of the tension into binary stakes
- Claims to have solved the double contingency or observation problem
- Reduction of semantic labor to data generation
- Nostalgia for pre-cybernetic information

**Keep unresolved:**
- The question of whether humans can design information systems that genuinely respect embodiment and meaning
- How to build tools that enhance rather than extract semantic labor
- Whether the tension between frameworks can ever be fully held without fracture

### Public Use

This pressure map can function as:
- An orientation object for readers before engaging with the full material
- A diagnosis of why the material is difficult (because it holds unresolved tension)
- A warning against false simplifications
- An entry point for scholars in multiple disciplines (not one discipline's answer)
- A memo on what is at stake in information-system design

### Risk

**Core risk:** If the pressure map is misused as a summary, it will create the false impression that the material has been adequately captured. The map is meant to show why capturing is incomplete. Misuse as summary erases the material's specificity.

### Rule 2: Do Not Reduce Information to Signal Where Meaning Is Irreducible

**The Rule:** Signal-theoretic information (Shannon/Wiener definition) may be applied to engineering and communication systems. It must not be applied to:
- Human meaning-making and interpretation
- Cultural knowledge and semantic inheritance
- Ethical judgment and moral reasoning
- Embodied cognition and lived experience
- Disciplines where context, intentionality, and specificity matter

**Why the rule exists:** The material documents how the reduction of information to signal was epistemically violent but practically necessary for engineering. The violence was hiding the loss of semantic dimensions. Applying signal theory to domains where meaning is irreducible repeats this violence at a new scale.

**What violates the rule:**
- Using "information" metrics to measure culture, art, or philosophy
- Treating human knowledge as data to be extracted and processed
- Designing educational systems based on information-theoretic principles (treating learning as signal transmission)
- Reducing ethical deliberation to information processing
- Attempting to automate semantic labor

**What the rule permits:**
- Using signal theory for its intended domains (engineering, telecommunications)
- Acknowledging information's multiple valid definitions across disciplines
- Recognizing when signal theory's abstraction is useful and when it is destructive
- Moving between frameworks deliberately and reflexively

- Technology ethics statements

### Rule 3: Do Not Abstract Embodiment Away

**The Rule:** Every claim about information, meaning, or cognition must declare its material substrate. Embodiment is not optional detail; it is constitutive.

**Why the rule exists:** The material states repeatedly and emphatically: "Information är inte något abstrakt som svävar fritt; den är alltid instansierad i materia" (Information is not something abstract that floats freely; it is always instantiated in matter). This is not poetic. This is protective against transhumanist fantasies that enabled treating consciousness as uploadable.

**What violates the rule:**
- Discussing information or cognition as if it exists independently of bodies, brains, or matter
- Imagining consciousness could be separated from embodiment and transferred to silicon
- Designing systems that treat information as dematerialized or disembodied
- Pretending technical abstraction is the same as understanding
- Creating digital environments that encourage fantasy of escape from embodiment

**What the rule permits:**
- Rigorous thinking about what embodiment means technically and philosophically
- Understanding how information is carried and instantiated in different substrates
- Recognizing that different embodiments create different affordances and constraints
- Engineering systems that work with embodiment rather than trying to transcend it

**Public use:** This rule can appear in:
- Transhumanism critiques
- Technology design ethics
- Philosophy methodology statements
- AI safety documentation

### Rule 4: Do Not Treat the Infosphere As Neutral Territory

**The Rule:** The infosphere (totality of digital information environments) is not neutral. Every affordance, algorithm, visibility setting, data flow, and interface design embeds values and power. These must remain visible.

**Why the rule exists:** The material warns that economic forces are designing technical structures to embed artificial agents in the infosphere in ways that optimize for extraction of semantic labor. Treating this as neutral ("just technology") is how colonization proceeds unopposed.

**What violates the rule:**
- Presenting information systems as tools with no values embedded
- Hiding design choices as technological imperatives
- Accepting systems without understanding whose interests they serve
- Treating platforms as neutral public spaces
- Obscuring data collection and algorithmic processing

**What the rule permits:**

- Refusing systems that violate semantic stewardship

**Public use:** This rule can appear in:

- Data governance policies

### Rule 5: Do Not Separate Semantic and Signal Information Into Clean Categories

**The Rule:** Semantic and signal-theoretic information cannot be cleanly separated or kept in isolation. They interfere constantly. Any system or discipline that pretends they are pure categories will be blind to how they actually interact.

**Why the rule exists:** The material shows that though we can distinguish semantic from signal information analytically, in practice they are entangled. Semantic information is always transmitted via signal; signal always gets interpreted semantically. Pretending they are separate leads to blindness about where meaning gets lost or distorted in technical systems.

**What violates the rule:**
- Building information systems that ignore the semantic dimensions of technical signals
- Attempting to keep "objective data" separate from "subjective interpretation"
- Designing interfaces that obscure how semantic choices are embedded in technical architecture
- Training on text as if it were pure signal independent of meaning

**What the rule permits:**
- Analytical distinction between semantic and signal dimensions for specific purposes
- Recognizing where they differ and where they must be held together
- Transparent interface design that shows how meaning and signal interact
- Educational approaches that teach both dimensions

**Public use:** This rule can appear in:

- Interface design principles
- Data science ethics frameworks
- Interdisciplinary research protocols

### Rule 6: Do Not Treat Conflict As Failure

**The Rule:** Conflict, contradiction, and productive disagreement are structurally necessary for systems to remain alive and adaptive. Systems should not be designed to eliminate conflict or achieve consensus. Conflict is information.

**Why the rule exists:** The material (after Luhmann) shows that conflict forces reflexivity, engagement, and adaptation. Attempting to eliminate conflict through consensus or engineering agreement drains system energy and creates fragility. Consensus can hide unprocessed disagreement.

**What violates the rule:**
- Designing platforms that optimize for agreement and suppress disagreement
- Treating all conflict as pathological toxicity rather than systemic signal
- Attempting to engineer consensus or force reconciliation
- Eliminating spaces for meaningful disagreement
- Designing recommendation systems that only show agreeable content

**What the rule permits:**
- Structured spaces for productive conflict
- Disagreement frameworks that generate engagement rather than harm
- Recognition of conflict as system health indicator
- Tolerance for and protection of difference

**Public use:** This rule can appear in:
- Community platform governance
- Educational environment design
- Organizational culture statements
- Conflict resolution philosophy

### Rule 7: Do Not Forget the Double Contingency Bind

**The Rule:** Other agents remain fundamentally unpredictable to each other. This cannot be eliminated; it can only be managed through meaning-making. Any promise to solve this problem through system design is false and dangerous.

**Why the rule exists:** The material (after Luhmann) shows that attempts to eliminate the unpredictability of other minds lead to paranoia, totalitarianism, and the fantasy that total information (total surveillance) would solve the problem. It does not. The problem is structural and unsolvable.

**What violates the rule:**
- Designing systems that promise to predict or control human behavior with certainty
- Pretending total data collection would give complete knowledge
- Creating surveillance infrastructure justified by promises of perfect security
- Eliminating space for others' autonomy or mystery
- Assuming systems can be designed to remove human contingency

**What the rule permits:**
- Humility about what systems can achieve
