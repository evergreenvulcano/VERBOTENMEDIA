Banketter

Ceremonial time, not updates

A bankett is a static render only after source, review, provenance, public-readiness, and ceremony all line up.

What a bankett is

A bankett is a ceremonial time marker for a work period, module, or apparatus. It can say what became possible without turning the work into a changelog, dashboard, launch note, or management surface.

It is not a raw archive and not a meeting note. It is a reviewed public rendering.

Doctrine: Bankett as time engine →

Render contract

A candidate bankett must move through a simple syscall-like sequence before it becomes a page: source exists, human/GPT review happens, provenance is recorded, protected boundaries are checked, public-readiness is decided, and only then a static render can be made.

This page defines the slot and the logic. It does not generate a new bankett package and does not import Drive ceremony material.

Known candidates

Two candidates are identified as public-bound in principle: the bankett celebrating Efterhandsmaskinen, explicitly not erotiska satelliter, and the bankett about astrologi and the radiokanal.

Neither text is rendered here yet. Promotion requires locating the actual source, reviewing it, and making a separate static-render decision.

What this avoids

No progress theatre, no archive dump, no content calendar, no ceremonial inflation. A bankett should let time appear without making the work behave like a product roadmap.
