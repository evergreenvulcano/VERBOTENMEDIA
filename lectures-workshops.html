Lectures & Workshops

Public Knowledge
and Collaborative Inquiry

Presentations and workshops on culture, technology, aesthetics, and contemporary life — for institutions, educational settings, and public audiences.

VERBOTEN MEDIA conducts lectures and workshops in educational and cultural contexts. The work is direct, intellectually rigorous, and calibrated to the specificity of each setting — neither a standard academic lecture nor a motivational presentation.

The focus is on developing forms of attention and critical literacy that are useful in environments saturated by technical mediation. The work addresses youth, professionals in cultural and educational fields, and broader public audiences.

Lecture

Living in the Feed: Identity, Attention, and Digital Self-Presentation

How contemporary platforms shape the experience of selfhood — not through overt persuasion but through the structure of participation itself. Examines the relationship between interface design, attention capture, and the available forms of identity expression. Suitable for secondary and higher education, cultural institutions, and general audiences.

Lecture

The Aesthetics of Now: Style, Surface, and Cultural Seriousness

A lecture on contemporary aesthetics as a form of thought — examining how the visual and formal languages of digital culture carry ideological content that is rarely made explicit. Addresses style as argument, surface as structure, and the conditions for cultural seriousness in an environment of accelerated image production.

Workshop

Reading the Interface: A Workshop in Critical Digital Literacy

A hands-on workshop that treats digital platforms as texts to be read — their grammar, assumptions, exclusions, and effects. Participants develop analytical frameworks for examining the interfaces they inhabit daily. Designed for groups of 8–20, adaptable for educational and professional contexts.

Workshop

Writing Against the Template: Form, Voice, and Resistance in Digital Environments

A writing workshop focused on developing a distinct voice and formal practice in conditions that reward conformity. Works through close reading, generative exercises, and collaborative critique. Suitable for writers, students, and practitioners working in any medium. Groups of up to 16.

Lecture · Workshop

Technology, Culture, and the Formation of Youth

A combined lecture and workshop examining how young people's relationship to technology, identity, and cultural life is being structured by forces that are rarely made legible to them. Addresses social form, peer culture, media literacy, and what education can and cannot do in technically saturated environments. For educators, youth workers, and policy audiences.

Seminar

Cultural Memory and the Digital Archive: What Survives and Why

An analytic seminar on the politics and aesthetics of digital memory — how platforms, institutions, and individuals decide what is preserved, what is allowed to disappear, and what the difference means. Suitable for cultural institutions, libraries, educational settings, and organizations working with heritage or documentation.

Lecture

Independent Cultural Infrastructure: Why It Exists and What It Costs

On the conditions of independent cultural production — what makes it possible, what forecloses it, and why it matters beyond the specific works it produces. Draws on VERBOTEN MEDIA's own experience and a broader comparative analysis of independent platforms in different national and economic contexts.

Workshop

Collaborative Creativity in Technical Environments

A workshop on working creatively in collaboration — within institutions, across disciplines, and under the conditions imposed by digital tools that often substitute coordination for genuine cooperation. Examines the social and psychological dynamics of collaborative work and offers practical frameworks for navigating them. For teams, collectives, and educational groups.
