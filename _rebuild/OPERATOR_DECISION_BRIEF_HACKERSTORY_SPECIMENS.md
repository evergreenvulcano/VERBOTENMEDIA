# Operator decision brief - HackerStory research specimens

Date: 2026-06-22
Branch: `codex/hackerstory-html-refactor-line`

## What changed

The HackerStory HTML collection has crossed from `_gpt/proposals/` into
`research-specimens/hackerstory/`. It is now linked from the landing page and
the Experimental Sandbox.

The publication frame names the collection as:

- fragmentary artifacts;
- AI-mediated research specimens;
- non-canonical process evidence;
- revisable and internally contradictory;
- an explicit opening of a previously protected boundary.

The source DOCX files and workstation-path audit remain outside the repo.

## Why this is not a normal promotion

The dossiers expose source-node names, generated interpretations, storyworld
figures, mutation ledgers, and speculative prose. They are not cleaned into
essays or made to impersonate finished research. Their instability is the
publication argument.

The repository is therefore allowed to change category through the act of
publishing them: it becomes not only a container for finished work and safe
afterimages, but also a host for bounded specimens of its own becoming.

## Decisions that remain live

1. Whether later HackerStory runs join this field or form separate specimen
   chambers.
2. Whether the specimen pages should eventually receive a shared site-level
   navigation bar or retain their autonomous visual editions.
3. Whether source-node names remain visible in future specimens by default or
   require an artifact-by-artifact decision.

## Recommendation

Keep the current editions autonomous and visibly unstable. Add future
specimens only when their publication changes what the repository can become,
not merely because another run exists.
