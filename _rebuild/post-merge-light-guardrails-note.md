## Seven light questions for future public-site passes

Ask only the questions that fit the task. Do not turn them into mandatory paperwork for every small fix.

1. **Is the target surface the actual surface named by the mandate?**

   If the task names `runtext.html`, `static-pages/writings/INDEX.md`, `banketter.html` or another specific file, do not let the work expand into navigation, CSS, README, docs, doctrine or adjacent pages unless a concrete technical blocker proves it necessary.

2. **Does the change preserve the current public hierarchy?**

   Verboten Media, XPRMNT-ZN, Underhållningsmode and Banketter remain primary public/work-facing nav bodies. Martin and Contact / Contribute remain responsibility and contact context under About. Do not recentre Martin, Contact or About as a dramatic entrance unless a separate human decision asks for it.

3. **Is this public-readable, public-ready, or being accidentally promoted?**

   Presence in `main` does not make an object canon, finished, primary or ready for a new public surface. If a file is source, staging, specimen, method figure, process artifact, ceremony, public working body or pointer, keep that status visible instead of solving uncertainty by promotion.

4. **Are links and routes still reachable without creating a new system?**

   For public edits, check that existing local links still resolve, no intended destination is orphaned, and no new hub, taxonomy, CMS, generator, issue template or route layer has been introduced to solve a local link problem.

5. **Does the change protect reading before explanation?**

   The site now has working entrances. Future changes should help a reader begin, continue or understand status with less friction. Do not replace that with onboarding, project ontology, generic "about us" copy, content strategy language or a defensive explanation layer.

6. **Does the change keep experimental forms from becoming products?**

   Underhållningsmode must not become feed, chatbot, CTA, tracking surface or personalized route. Praktikanten must not become bot, guide, persona, NPC, mailpersona, dashboard, onboarding surface or continuing service. Runtext should remain an experimental reading form, not a recommendation engine.

7. **Is the Bankett or XPRMNT-ZN impulse mature enough to act?**

   Banketter should mark ceremony-bearing time, not calendar pressure. XPRMNT-ZN should remain a membrane for selected public bodies, not a Drive mirror, product portal, complete catalog or parking lot for exciting material. If maturity or public-readiness is unclear, stop at diagnosis or human review.

## Use

For a future small public-site pass, this note is enough:

- answer the relevant questions briefly in the final report;
- validate links or rendering in proportion to risk;
- leave unrelated surfaces alone;
- stop when the named surface is handled.

If these questions start generating new files, registers, doctrine or standing obligations, they have outgrown their purpose.
