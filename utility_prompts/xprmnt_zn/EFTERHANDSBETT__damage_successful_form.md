# EFTERHANDSBETT

Status: PUBLISHED_METHOD_OBJECT / EXPERIMENTAL_UTILITY / NON_DEFAULT

Scope: XPRMNT-ZN / mutation utility

Purpose: Damage a successful form before it becomes profile, method, merit, or controlled wildness.

## Use When

Use this when an XPRMNT-ZN output feels strong but too usable.

This utility does not improve the text.

It asks who gains power when the text becomes readable.

## Input

- Successful form:
- Why it works:
- Where it risks becoming merit:
- What concrete detail refuses usefulness:
- What low object can interrupt it:
- What ending would make the success less clean:

## Procedure

1. Locate the proudest part of the form.
2. Ask what it has started to sell, prove, or authorize.
3. Find one practical detail that does not care.
4. Let that detail act.
5. Compress the result into one ödesrad.

## Output

- One ödesrad.
- One contamination note.
- One possible next scene-risk.

## Rule

Do not explain the critique after the line.

The sentence must carry the damage.

## Example

A manifesto learned to limp, refused every slogan offered to it, crawled through three correct refusals, and was finally stopped by the receipt it forgot to throw away.

## Failure Mode

If the line sounds like a moral lesson, remove the moral.

If the object becomes metaphor, make it more practical.

If the form remains proud, bite harder.
